## Lessons

### L01 — DSR-passing IC does not equal deployable strategy

**Rule.** A signal that clears IC + DSR + plateau + sanctuary is NOT guaranteed to produce a deployable backtest. The cost-aware engine can still kill the edge.

**Why.** `intraday_range_atr` on NQ/ES H1 cleared every Phase C IC gate (TIER_B). The Phase 0 strategy backtest at 2+1 bps spread + IBKR commission produced negative Sharpe on all 5 grid cells with CI_lo well below zero. Per-trade alpha (~$1-25 on ES) ≈ round-trip cost (~$6-25 on ES). Cost/alpha ratio > 1.

**How to apply.** For every IC-survivor proposed as a strategy: compute `alpha_per_trade / cost_per_trade` BEFORE running the full backtest. If < 1, the strategy is cost-dominated regardless of the IC.

**Source:** Range-Expansion Phase 0 strategy backtest (NQ + ES H1, ATR-period sweep). Archived in V2.0 cleanse 2026-05-14.

### L02 — Raw IC peak is not the strategy-engine peak when the strategy has multiplicative layers

**Rule.** A signal whose raw IC peaks at parameter X may produce its best Sharpe at a different parameter Y when the strategy uses the signal through multiplicative layers (regime gate, percentile tiers, vol-target sizing).

**Why.** `mr_audjpy` lives at `vwap_anchor=24`. IC fine-grid showed monotonic IC decay; anchor=6 has 4× the raw IC magnitude of anchor=24. The full strategy backtest at anchor=6 produced Sharpe +0.10 / CI_lo -0.41 (worse than random); anchor=24 produced +0.77 / CI_lo +0.32. The strategy's tier-percentile + regime + sizing layers were tuned for anchor=24's slower signal.

**How to apply.** Don't decide config changes from IC analysis alone. Run the FULL strategy backtest at the candidate parameter, compare CI_lo to the live cell. Only flip if the candidate's CI_lo beats the live by ≥ 0.20.

**Source:** MR AUDJPY anchor=24 vs anchor=6 audit re-run. Archived in V2.0 cleanse 2026-05-14.

### L03 — Sanctuary-included Sharpe is not deployable Sharpe

**Rule.** Treat the last 12 months as forbidden until a held-out one-shot sanctuary pass. Any "OOS" Sharpe that included the most recent year was IS-with-extra-time, not true OOS.

**Why.** IHYG → VUSD published claim was +1.16 / CI_lo +0.47. Under audit-corrected math WITH the last 12 months held out, the same strategy produces +0.36 / CI_lo -0.235 (CI flipped negative). The recent year was a particularly strong period; bundling it into OOS made the strategy look more robust than it is.

**How to apply.** Sanctuary slicing is mandatory and standardised at `df[df.index < df.index[-1] - DateOffset(months=12)]`. Sanctuary pass is separate (one-shot, not weighted into the gate). A positive sanctuary Sharpe does NOT override a failing pre-sanctuary WFO.

**Source:** Bond-Equity audit (IHYU→CSPX / IHYG→VUSD / IHYG→EMIM) with DSR + MC + sanctuary. Archived in V2.0 cleanse 2026-05-14.

### L04 — Look-ahead in alignment is bounded by position persistence

**Rule.** Same-bar `position * bar_return` without `.shift(1)` is a real look-ahead bug. Its quantitative impact at the WFO-stitched level is proportional to **transition frequency** — if positions are persistent (held across many bars), the bias is small.

**Why.** `run_52signal_classifier.py:613` had this bug. Audit found inflation of -2.6% (EUR/USD; noise wins) to +7.4% (QQQ) at the stitched OOS level. `_pred_to_position` holds until prediction flips, so transition bars are rare. For per-bar-fresh-decision strategies (e.g. high-frequency tier-aware) the bias would be much larger.

**How to apply.** Fix the bug regardless. But quantify the inflation per strategy before assuming it dominates. A high-conviction strategy with persistent positions may survive the fix; a high-turnover strategy may collapse.

**Source:** ML Tier A same-bar causality audit (EUR/USD D, QQQ D). Archived in V2.0 cleanse 2026-05-14.

### L05 — CI_lo gates are stricter than point-estimate Sharpe gates

**Rule.** A strategy can have a strong point-estimate Sharpe (+1.5) and still fail the bootstrap-CI gate (CI_lo ≤ 0). Wide CI usually means small N (insufficient WFO folds or short OOS), and the right fix is more data, not relaxing the gate.

**Why.** ML Tier A EUR/USD D had causal-aligned Sharpe = +1.62, CI = [-0.10, +3.19]. The point estimate matches the published claim; the CI says the sample is too thin to deploy. Per April 2026 audit policy: `tier = unconfirmed`.

**How to apply.** Always report Sharpe alongside CI_lo, never alone. A point Sharpe > 1.0 with CI_lo ≤ 0 is `tier = unconfirmed` until N is increased.

**Source:** ML Tier A same-bar causality audit — bootstrap CI on stitched OOS. Archived in V2.0 cleanse 2026-05-14.

### L06 — Sparse-trade per-bar Sharpe annualisation produces extreme magnitudes

**Rule.** For sparse-trade strategies (intraday breakouts, ML classifiers that hold for many bars), computing Sharpe on the per-bar P&L series and annualising at `BARS_PER_YEAR` amplifies any per-trade bias by `sqrt(BARS_PER_YEAR)`. Report **per-trade Sharpe** with `trades_per_year` as the primary metric instead.

**Why.** ORB DSR audit produced Sharpe -16 to +5 across 9 instruments on ~15-25 trades per ticker over 3 months at M5 frequency. Annualising at `sqrt(78*252)` = 140× magnifies any per-trade bias. The numbers don't reflect strategy economics.

**How to apply.** Use the per-class typology (`titan.research.framework.typology.DEFAULTS`) to pick the right Sharpe convention. `INTRADAY_BREAKOUT` → per-trade primary. `INTRADAY_MICROSTRUCTURE` → per-bar primary (it's NOT sparse — positions are held continuously). Read the typology table before reporting Sharpe.

**Source:** ORB DSR audit at N=482 — sparse-trade M5 Sharpe annualisation. Archived in V2.0 cleanse 2026-05-14.

### L07 — N for DSR is the full screener pool, not the survivors

**Rule.** When applying DSR after a screening process, `N` is the size of the candidate pool (e.g. 482 for the warrior screener), and `sr_var_across_trials` should ideally come from the FULL pool's Sharpe distribution. Using only survivors UNDERSTATES `sr_var` → OPTIMISTIC DSR-prob.

**Why.** ORB DSR audit used survivors-only `sr_var = 7.4²`. Even at that optimistic value, e_max_SR = 22.5 and all 9 cells fail. With the true (full-pool) sr_var, the gate would fail by even more. Survivor-only is documented as a lower bound.

**How to apply.** Always pass `survivors_only=True` to `deflated_sharpe` if the variance comes only from survivors. The result is flagged as optimistic. Run the screener on a random subset of the universe to estimate the true sr_var when feasible.

**Source:** ORB DSR audit at N=482 — survivors-only sr_var as lower bound. Archived in V2.0 cleanse 2026-05-14.

### L08 — MC tail-risk gates must be calibrated per strategy class

**Rule.** A single P(MaxDD > X) < Y threshold doesn't fit all strategy classes. Always-on cross-asset momentum strategies inherit the underlying's tail risk; intraday-sparse strategies don't.

**Why.** Bond-equity audit at P(MaxDD > 25%) < 5% failed all 6 cells including audit-trusted references (TLT→QQQ, IEF→GLD, HYG→IWB). The gate was structurally too tight for always-on cross-asset strategies — ~half of random-shuffle MC paths hit 25% MaxDD purely from the unconditional vol structure of equity-long positions.

**How to apply.** Use class-specific MC thresholds from `titan.research.framework.typology.DEFAULTS[cls].mc`. For `CROSS_ASSET_MOMENTUM` the framework-recalibrated gate is P(MaxDD > 35%) < 10%. Don't pre-commit a tighter gate without mechanistic justification.

**Source:** Bond-Equity audit — MC gate broken for cross-asset momentum. Archived in V2.0 cleanse 2026-05-14. Now baked into `titan.research.framework.typology.DEFAULTS[StrategyClass.CROSS_ASSET_MOMENTUM].mc`.

### L09 — Pre-committed decision matrices must cover all empirical outcomes

**Rule.** Every empirical input vector to the audit's decision matrix must map deterministically to a verdict. UNDETERMINED outcomes mean the matrix is incomplete; that's a methodology bug.

**Why.** ML Tier A audit hit UNDETERMINED because the pre-committed matrix had no row for "small inflation AND high Sharpe AND negative CI_lo". The framework's 4-axis decision matrix (`titan.research.framework.decision.decide`) eliminates this by construction: every 4-tuple in `{best, mid, worst}^4` maps to a verdict.

**How to apply.** Use `titan.research.framework.decision.decide(DecisionInputs(...))` instead of inventing custom matrices per directive. Strategy-specific threshold overrides go through `GateThresholds`.

**Source:** ML Tier A audit hit UNDETERMINED on the pre-committed matrix. Archived in V2.0 cleanse 2026-05-14. The framework's `titan.research.framework.decision.decide` eliminates UNDETERMINED by construction.

### L10 — Simulators that approximate the live class don't validate the live class

**Rule.** A simulator that uses simpler context filters (SMA proxy for Gaussian Channel, fixed costs for variable spreads) cannot produce per-cell verdicts that justify retiring the live strategy.

**Why.** ORB DSR audit used SMA(50) for `use_sma`, SMA(20) for `use_gauss`. Per-cell Sharpe -16 to +5 -- but the live class uses an exact Gaussian Channel, ticker-specific time-of-day filters, and per-bar fill simulation. The simulator's verdicts are at the CLASS level (selection-bias structural concern), not the cell level.

**How to apply.** For per-cell audit verdicts, use the ACTUAL live class (import `titan.strategies.*.strategy`) in a backtest harness. Simulators are fine for structural / methodological tests, not for retire/keep decisions on individual instruments.

**Source:** ORB DSR audit — simulator vs live class gap noted. Archived in V2.0 cleanse 2026-05-14.

### L11 — Data-source overwrites must be guarded

**Rule.** A downloader that writes `data/{symbol}_{tf}.parquet` may silently overwrite a longer-history file from a different source. Catch this BEFORE the audit runs.

**Why.** The IBKR VIX downloader overwrote `VIX_D.parquet` (yfinance, 2000-2026) with a shorter IBKR-sourced D file (2006-2026). Phase B reproducibility was broken until manually caught + restored.

**How to apply.** Per-source downloaders either (a) write to a source-specific filename (e.g. `VIX_D_ibkr.parquet`) and have a resolution rule for the canonical file, or (b) check the existing file's date range and refuse to overwrite if the new file's history is shorter. The H1-only patch to the IBKR script is option (b).

**Source:** Phase C session, IBKR downloader regression

### L12 — API defaults can silently constrain results

**Rule.** Library API defaults (e.g. `end=None`) may not mean "end of available data" — they may mean "use the start as the only date". Always pass explicit boundaries and verify against expected output size.

**Why.** Databento `metadata.get_cost` and `timeseries.get_range` with `end=None` returned a SINGLE-DAY result, not the full range to today. First Phase C download produced 1 bar per parquet at $0.0001 cost; corrected to 2,187+ bars at $5.50 cost after the bug was caught.

**How to apply.** Always pass explicit `end = pd.Timestamp.utcnow().strftime("%Y-%m-%d")` (or specific date). Verify output bar count against expected sample size at first invocation.

**Source:** Phase C session, Databento downloader bug

### L13 — Fold quorum must exceed the pigeonhole minimum

**Rule.** For 5-fold sign-stability, the quorum threshold must be ≥ 4. Any threshold ≤ 3 is **tautological** — by pigeonhole, any 5 non-NaN folds have ≥ 3 sharing a sign.

**Why.** `fold_ic_signs` originally hard-coded `max(3, n_folds // 2 + 1) = 3` for 5 folds. Every cell trivially passed. Caught when the user spotted the TOML's `fold_sign_quorum = 4` was being ignored by the code. Fix made the gate non-trivial and adds a near-zero-IC carve-out (|IC| < 0.005 = sign-agnostic).

**How to apply.** Use `quorum = 4` for n_folds=5; the carve-out for NaN / near-zero folds is the right way to handle data-sparse folds. Generalise: `quorum > n_folds // 2 + 1`.

**Source:** Phase A session, `ic_census_lib.fold_ic_signs` fix

### L14 — Anchored MTF rule + assert_causal must wrap every cross-TF aggregation

**Rule.** When a higher-TF signal feeds a lower-TF strategy (or vice versa), apply `anchored_aggregate(..., higher_tf=False)` which is `.shift(1)` + reindex with ffill onto the lower-TF index. Wrap the aggregation in `assert_causal` (corrupt-the-future test) to verify causality programmatically.

**Why.** The original EUR/USD MTF +1.94 Sharpe was invalidated by ffill of higher-TF signals into past lower-TF bars (the canonical look-ahead pattern). IC Census Phase A used this primitive + smoke test from day one; Phase B + C cross-asset signals all went through it.

**How to apply.** Use `titan.research.framework`'s framework or `research.ic_analysis.ic_census_lib.anchored_aggregate` + `assert_causal`. Never `.ffill()` on a cross-TF series without prior `.shift(1)`.

**Source:** IC Signal Census Phase A — Anchored MTF Aggregation Rule + causality smoke test. Archived in V2.0 cleanse 2026-05-14. The primitives now live in `research/ic_analysis/ic_census_lib.py::anchored_aggregate` + `assert_causal`.

### L15 — A positive sanctuary cannot rescue a failing WFO

**Rule.** If the WFO stitched OOS Sharpe has CI_lo ≤ 0, the strategy is `tier = unconfirmed` regardless of whether the sanctuary pass is positive. The sanctuary is a verification step, not an alternate gate.

**Why.** Multiple cases this session: range-expansion (WFO CI_lo all negative, sanctuary +1.24 / +1.66); IHYG → VUSD (WFO CI_lo -0.235, sanctuary +2.17); IHYG → EMIM (similar). Each tempted "let's deploy on the sanctuary's strength" — V3.1 says no.

**How to apply.** The framework's 4-axis decision matrix gives the sanctuary axis one vote, equal to the others. It cannot single-handedly elevate a 0-of-4 outcome to DEPLOY. The asymmetry is intentional.

**Source:** Range-Expansion Phase 0 + Bond-Equity audit — both showed sanctuary >> WFO. Archived in V2.0 cleanse 2026-05-14.

### L17 — Absolute P(MaxDD>X) MC gate fails for long-only equity strategies

**Rule.** For any long-only equity (or other always-on / single-asset-class) strategy, the **absolute** Monte Carlo gate (P(MaxDD > class threshold) < class pass-prob) is the wrong test. Use the **relative** MC gate instead: compare the strategy's MaxDD distribution to a benchmark's (typically buy-and-hold the primary underlying) on the **same** block-bootstrap paths.

**Pass criterion:**
- median(strategy_MaxDD / benchmark_MaxDD) ≤ 0.80 (strategy reduces median MaxDD by ≥20% vs benchmark), AND
- fraction of paths where strategy_MaxDD ≥ benchmark_MaxDD (i.e. less negative) ≥ 0.50 (strategy is at least as good on the majority of paths).

**Why.** Block-bootstrap of 20+ years of underlying returns shuffles real crisis bars (2008, 2020, 2022) into the majority of synthetic paths. The underlying itself can't pass an absolute 35% gate under this construction (empirically: buy-hold SPY P(MaxDD>35%) = 0.80 in our framework). Any long-only strategy on the same underlying inherits this floor. An absolute gate then rejects every strategy whose thesis is "I drawdown less than the underlying" — including strategies that genuinely add defensive value.

The relative gate asks the correct economic question: **does the strategy's regime gate / ballast / overlay actually reduce drawdown vs the underlying on the same path?** A strategy whose defensive switch helps will pass; one whose switch is timed too slowly (or whose lookback can't react to randomised regime structure) will fail. Both are honest verdicts.

**How to apply.**
1. Implementation: `titan.research.framework.run_relative_block_mc(primary_close, cfg, strategy_fn, benchmark_fn, ...)`.
2. Benchmark choice: always-long primary underlying for long-only strategies; cash (Series of zeros) for market-neutral overlays; the underlying basket for cross-asset rotations.
3. Wire the relative gate's `median_dd_reduction` into the 4-axis decision matrix's MC axis (the synthetic mapping: gate becomes the `pass_threshold_prob`, ratio becomes the `p_maxdd_gt_threshold`).
4. Continue to report the absolute MC for diagnostic context (it tells you the underlying's bootstrap tail) but **don't** make the verdict turn on it.

**Source.** GEM Dual Momentum audit 2026-05-14 (`directives/Pre-Reg GEM Dual Momentum 2026-05-14.md` §4). The pre-reg's MC axis used the class-default `CROSS_ASSET_MOMENTUM` gate of P(MaxDD>35%) < 10%. Result: all 5 cells failed the absolute gate; SPY-buy-and-hold also failed it (P=0.80). Replacing with the relative gate gave the proper verdict (CONDITIONAL_WATCHPOINT: the strategy's defensive switch is load-bearing — confirmed via C5 ablation — but doesn't reduce MaxDD enough under randomised paths to clear the 0.80 ratio gate). Implementation added to `titan/research/framework/mc.py` in the same commit.

### L18 — Stateful buffer must compare against LIVE incumbent values, not frozen snapshots

**Rule.** When a strategy uses a stateful buffer ("don't switch unless challenger beats incumbent by X"), the comparison must use the incumbent's CURRENT signal value at decision time -- never a value snapshotted at the last switch. Cache only the IDENTITY of the incumbent (so we know it's the same pick); look up its current return fresh each bar.

**Why.** A buffer that compares challenger-now vs incumbent-then can latch a strategy in a position that never wants to leave. The frozen snapshot drifts arbitrarily far from current reality; the gate is then asking "is the new option better than this OLD HIGH WATER MARK?" rather than "is the new option better than what we're currently in?"

**How to apply.** Refactor buffer logic to one-line discipline: `if challenger_value - lookup(incumbent_id, current_t) >= buffer: switch`. Add a regression test that builds a bull → bear synthetic and asserts the defensive switch fires (would have failed under the bug).

**Source.** GEM Step 1 (2026-05-15): the original buffer cached `current_ret = challenger_ret` at switch time and never updated. Real data trace: at end-November 2007 the buffer locked EFA's +17.46% return as the comparator; the strategy held EFA throughout 2008 (-42% from peak) because IEF's actual 12m return never re-reached that stale +17.46% threshold. Defensive switch effectively disabled for the entire GFC. Fix: compare against `incumbent's CURRENT 12m return` at every decision bar.

### L19 — Continuous vol-targeting beats binary regime-on/off stress gating

**Rule.** For a vol-targeted long-only strategy, prefer a CONTINUOUS scale-by-vol overlay over a BINARY stress-on/off gate. Smooth scaling tracks the actual vol path; binary gating only fires when an arbitrary threshold is crossed and ignores the path between thresholds.

**Why.** Continuous vol-target keeps the strategy at low vol always; binary gating lets the strategy run hot during "calm" windows that turn out to have positive drift but high variance within the gate's blind zones. Empirically (GEM Step 4): switching from continuous (C8) to gated (C9) cost 0.14 Sharpe at the same vol budget. The path-of-vol matters, not just the regime label.

**How to apply.** Default to continuous vol-scaling. Add stress-gating only if the use case explicitly demands ON/OFF behaviour (e.g., for total-return preservation in calm periods where you DON'T want to scale down). Run BOTH variants under the framework's L17 relative-MC and compare per-axis.

**Source.** GEM Step 4 (2026-05-15): cells C9-C11 (stress-gated) underperformed C8 (continuous) on every axis: Sharpe (0.71 vs 0.84), CI lo (0.30 vs 0.45), rel-MC ratio (0.67 vs 0.52), sanctuary (0.62 vs 1.07). All cells passed DEPLOY but C8 dominated.

### L20 — Cross-source signal merges require date-only index normalisation

**Rule.** When loading multiple daily series from different parquet sources (e.g. primary close from one vendor, regime indicator from another), normalise EVERY index to date-only (00:00 time-of-day) before any reindex / merge / shift operation.

**Why.** Different vendors timestamp daily bars at different times-of-day (yfinance uses 04:00, IBKR daily bars use 05:00, Databento may use 00:00, etc.). Without normalisation, `regime_series.reindex(primary.index)` produces an all-NaN series because the timestamps never exactly match. The downstream signal silently "fails open" -- e.g. `(regime_series > threshold)` returns all False, so the regime gate never fires. This is a SILENT bug: nothing crashes, the framework just thinks the regime never triggered.

**How to apply.** In data loaders: `s.index = pd.to_datetime(s.index).tz_localize(None).normalize()`. The `.normalize()` strips the time-of-day, leaving date-only DatetimeIndex. All daily series in the project should follow this convention.

**Source.** GEM Step 7 (2026-05-15): the C11 cell with `stress_vix_threshold=25.0` was bit-exact identical to C10 (no VIX). Diagnosis: SPY parquet stamped 04:00 EDT, VIX parquet stamped 05:00 EDT. `vix.reindex(spy.index)` returned all NaN; the VIX > 25 check never fired. Pre-fix: VIX threshold contributed 0 stress bars. Post-fix: 942 / 5850 stress bars (16% of history; matches the 1270 days SPY index has had VIX > 25). Verdict was unaffected (VIX is redundant with realised vol over the rel-MC gate), but the bug was real.

### L21 — Realistic transaction costs are non-load-bearing for slow-turnover strategies

**Rule.** Apply realistic transaction costs (e.g. 1.5 bps/turnover for US ETFs) to every audit as a matter of discipline. But don't gate the verdict on a tiny cost-induced Sharpe drop -- for slow-rebalance strategies (monthly or slower) the impact is typically < 0.05 on Sharpe.

**Why.** Honesty: every published Sharpe should be cost-adjusted. Discipline: catching a strategy where costs DO matter (high-turnover MR, vol-target overlays with daily nudges) is what the gate is for. Empirically: GEM C8 with vol-target overlay produces ~0.01 Sharpe drag from 1.5 bps/turnover -- effectively unmeasurable.

**How to apply.** Add `cost_bps_per_turnover` to the strategy's return function. Default 0 (legacy). The audit harness sets 1.5 (US-ETF realistic) or 1.0 (CME futures realistic). Note the rate used in §4 result log. Distinct from the cost gate -- this is informational + verdict-stability, not a separate axis.

**Source.** GEM Step 6 (2026-05-15): adding 1.5 bps/turnover dropped C8 Sharpe from +0.85 (costless) to +0.84 (with costs). Same rank-ordering across all 15 cells. Verdict unaffected.

### L23 — Cost models must include the commission floor and a rebalance threshold, not just bps/turnover

**Rule.** A pure `cost_bps_per_turnover` model under-prices reality whenever any of: (a) notional is small (<$50k per strategy), (b) the broker charges a per-fill commission floor (IBKR Lite $4, Pro $1), or (c) a vol-target overlay produces many small daily weight tweaks. Three corrections are non-optional:

1. **Per-leg variable cost.** ETF leg ~6 bps/turnover (spread+slip); MES futures ~1 bps/turnover. A leveraged strategy that "uses MES for the SPY leg" must split the cost: SPY → MES rate, EFA/IEF → ETF rate. Single-rate models systematically over- or under-price one leg.
2. **Per-fill commission floor.** Add a fixed `usd_per_fill / notional` charge on any day with non-zero turnover. At $30k notional this is 3.3 bps/fill (Pro) or 13.3 bps/fill (Lite) — dominates the bps-rate term for vol-target strategies that fill ~45 days/year.
3. **Live rebalance threshold in the backtest.** If the live class only submits orders when `|w_new - w_old| > threshold` (GEM's `rebalance_threshold_weight=0.05`), apply the same filter in the backtest. Otherwise the model counts sub-threshold daily vol-target tweaks the live class would have skipped, and either over-charges the bps-rate term or under-charges the per-fill term depending on calibration.

**Why.** L21 said "costs are non-load-bearing for slow-turnover strategies" and that was correct for the cost MODEL we had — 1.5 bps × ~10 turnover/year = 15 bps drag, negligible. But the live audit on the same paper account (directives/Cost Model Audit 2026-05-11.md) found *real* drag was 5× the modelled drag. The error was structural: missing commission floor + ETF-class miscalibration + no rebalance threshold. GEM C12 realistic ETF-mode drag = 87 bps/yr; MES-mode drag = 67 bps/yr. Still doesn't kill the verdict but it is no longer negligible.

**How to apply.** When porting to a new strategy: (i) take a calibration sample from real fills on the target broker/account, (ii) split variable cost by leg if the strategy mixes ETFs with futures, (iii) always pass a per-fill USD floor plus the live rebalance threshold. Re-run the audit before deploying. Costs that look like 18 bps/yr in the legacy model may be 90 bps/yr in reality.

**Source.** GEM cost-model audit 2026-05-15 (this commit). Live IBKR audit 2026-05-11 (`bond_equity` strategies). Numbers: legacy model 18 bps/yr, recalibrated ETF model 87 bps/yr, recalibrated MES model 67 bps/yr on GEM C12 over 15.7 years of UK UCITS data.

### L22 — Drawdown circuit breaker is redundant on top of vol-targeting

**Rule.** A drawdown circuit breaker (cut position at -10% DD, flatten at -15%) adds marginal value when stacked on a vol-targeted strategy. Vol-targeting keeps daily vol bounded; bounded daily vol implies bounded peak-to-trough drawdown; the DD breaker rarely fires and slightly drags Sharpe via late-cycle haircuts that miss recovery.

**Why.** DD breakers are most useful for strategies without explicit risk control where path-dependent drawdowns can compound. Once a vol-target is in place, the breaker becomes a second-order corrector that occasionally cuts exposure during a recovery (because DD takes time to mean-revert above the recovery threshold even after vol normalises).

**How to apply.** Don't reflexively add DD breakers to vol-targeted strategies. If the breaker IS desired (e.g., for compliance / risk-budget reasons), expect a small Sharpe drag (~0.02 in GEM C14 vs C8). For strategies WITHOUT vol-targeting, the breaker is more useful -- run an A/B in the audit.

**Source.** GEM Step 5 (2026-05-15): C14 (C8 + DD breaker) produced Sharpe +0.82 vs C8 +0.84 (drag of 0.02), with slightly worse sanctuary (+0.71 vs +1.07 -- the breaker fired in the sanctuary period and missed some recovery). MaxDD essentially unchanged.

### L24 — Noise-injection robustness is the 5th decision axis, not a side panel

**Rule.** The Varma noise-injection robustness gate (`run_noise_robustness(...)`) is a load-bearing axis of the decision matrix, not a diagnostic side panel. A strategy that passes CI_lo + DSR + MC + Sanctuary but fails the noise gate is `CONDITIONAL_WATCHPOINT`, NOT `DEPLOY`. A strategy that passes mean degradation but fails worst-case is `mid` on the axis — production-eligible only if every other axis is `best`.

**Why.** Pre-J3, the noise gate ran standalone in audits (e.g. GEM Step 3 ran it on the canonical cell, found PASS, reported it in the result log, did NOT feed it to `decide()`). This meant a strategy fragile to small input-price noise could still earn a `DEPLOY` verdict from the 4-axis matrix as long as its statistical confidence axes were strong. That is the exact failure mode Varma's "Signal vs Noise" warns about: lucky-pattern fits look great on the realised price path, evaporate under perturbation. The 4-axis matrix did not catch this class of overfit because none of its other axes test input-path sensitivity. V3.2's plateau rule catches it ONLY when the operator runs a ±1-step grid sweep around the canonical cell, which not all audits do (and the framework cannot guarantee they did).

**How to apply.**

1. Every audit harness MUST call `run_noise_robustness(visible, strategy_fn, periods_per_year=..., cfg=NoiseConfig(...))` PER CELL on the same visible window the stitched OOS was computed on.
2. Feed `noise_result.passes` (mean) and `noise_result.worst_case_passes` to `DecisionInputs.noise_passes_mean` and `noise_passes_worst`. The framework computes the axis level (best/mid/worst) and assembles the 5-axis verdict.
3. Report the noise column in the §4 result log alongside the other axes. Note any cell where the noise axis is `worst` — that's a non-deployable cell regardless of how good the other 4 axes look.
4. The 5-axis verdict map: 5/5→DEPLOY, 4/5→CONDITIONAL_WATCHPOINT, 3/5→TIER_UNCONFIRMED, 2/5→SUSPECT, 0–1/5→RETIRE.
5. New strategies are `DEPLOY`-eligible only when ALL 5 axes are `best`. A `CONDITIONAL_WATCHPOINT` cell with the noise axis as the failing one is NOT promoted to production (the failure mode is algorithmic, not operational — no monitoring threshold can rescue a fragile strategy).

**Source.** Pre-Reg J3 Noise Robustness 5th Axis 2026-05-15. Code changes: `titan/research/framework/decision.py` (5-axis `decide()`), `titan/research/framework/__init__.py` (export `classify_axis_noise`), `tests/test_framework_synthetic.py` (243-cell totality), `research/gem/run_gem_audit.py` (per-cell noise gate fed to `DecisionInputs`).

### L25 — MC gate thresholds must be calibrated per exposure type, not just per StrategyClass

**Rule.** The `StrategyClass` typology assigns one MC threshold pair per class (e.g. `DAILY_MEAN_REVERSION` defaults to `P(MaxDD > 25%) < 10%`). But the same `StrategyClass` can include strategies whose natural drawdown distribution is fundamentally different — long/short oscillator equity mean-reversion has different tail risk from short-volatility carry. A one-size-fits-all class threshold is systematically too tight for the heavier-tail exposure type, producing forced FAIL verdicts on legitimate strategies.

**Why.** VRP capture (E1, 2026-05-15) — short VIXY in contango regime — was audited under `DAILY_MEAN_REVERSION` defaults. All 7 cells hit `P(MaxDD > 25%) = 1.0` under block-bootstrap MC, because VIXY paths routinely produce 30–60% drawdowns even under normal contango. The literature (Eraker-Wu, *JFE* 2017; Cheng SSRN 2020) reports MaxDDs in the 30–70% range as the structural cost of VRP harvesting — that's not a strategy failure mode, that's the trade. The class default treated it as a verdict-blocking failure.

**How to apply.**

1. When the strategy lives in a class but with a fundamentally different exposure profile from the class's canonical examples (vol carry inside `DAILY_MEAN_REVERSION`; futures basket inside `CARRY`; basket pairs inside `PAIRS`), pre-register a **class-default override** with rationale in §3 of the pre-reg.
2. Override format: state the new threshold pair (`P(MaxDD > X) < Y`) and cite the empirical / literature basis for it.
3. Long-term solution (medium-priority backlog item): sub-class the typology so `DAILY_MEAN_REVERSION_VOL_CARRY`, `CARRY_FUTURES_BASKET`, etc., each have their own MC defaults. Until then, per-strategy pre-reg overrides are the discipline mechanism.

**Source.** VRP Capture audit E1, 2026-05-15: every cell forced SUSPECT primarily by the MC axis, despite point Sharpe +0.21 to +0.46 and DSR + Sanctuary axes both passing. The class default fits oscillator MR-on-equities, not short-vol carry.

### L26 — Threshold-based regime gates are structurally noise-fragile

**Rule.** Any regime-switch logic of the form `if signal[t-1] >= threshold: state = A else: state = B` will fail the Varma noise-injection gate (L24) unless the threshold sits well inside a parameter plateau (V3.2). Small input noise on the signal will flip the regime decision on bars near the threshold, and these flips are uncorrelated with edge — they are pure noise harvested by the strategy as costs. The result: a strategy whose realised Sharpe depends on the specific input price path, not on a durable signal.

**Why.** VRP Capture E1: every one of 7 cells failed the noise axis (degradation > 30% at 0.5σ noise). Cells varied the threshold value, the buffer, the position size, and the overlay — all 7 failed. Variation in base Sharpe across cells (0.21 → 0.46) was 2×, but the noise-degradation gate failed uniformly. This is the textbook parameter-spike-not-plateau pattern (V3.2) made visible by L24's noise axis. The fragility class is structural, not parameterisable away.

**How to apply.**

1. **Don't use bare-threshold regime gates** in new strategies. If you have signal `s`, do NOT write `regime = s >= K`. Instead choose ONE of the mitigations below.
2. **Percentile-based gates** (preferred for time-varying signals): `regime = s >= rolling_percentile(s, window=252, q=0.60)`. The gate moves with the signal's own distribution, so a 0.1σ noise injection does not systematically shift it.
3. **Multi-gate ensemble**: compute regime votes at K different threshold values around the canonical K (e.g. K-2%, K-1%, K, K+1%, K+2%), and require ≥ 3 of 5 votes to agree before entering the regime. Effectively a built-in plateau check.
4. **Continuous scaling**: replace step-function `position = w_target * (s >= K)` with `position = w_target * sigmoid((s - K) / scale)`. The strategy fades into the regime instead of jumping; noise near K perturbs the position slightly instead of flipping it 100%.
5. **Pre-flight check**: before running the full audit, sweep ±1 step around the canonical threshold and report the per-cell Sharpe spread. If spread > 30% across the neighbourhood, the design has a noise-fragility risk and one of the mitigations above should be applied at design time, BEFORE the audit.

**Source.** VRP Capture audit E1, 2026-05-15: regime gates at `ratio_long >= 1.05` (and variants 1.02, 1.10) all fail noise. Per-cell Sharpe spread 54% across the grid — far outside the V3.2 plateau gate of 30%.

### L27 — Plateau pre-flight runs BEFORE the audit, not after

**Rule.** Run the V3.2 plateau check (canonical cell + 6 grid neighbours) at the START of an audit harness, BEFORE the per-cell MC + noise + DSR pipeline. If the canonical's ±1-step neighbours have a relative Sharpe spread > 30%, abort the audit and report the spread. The design has no robust parameter region; spending compute on the full pipeline is wasted.

**Why.** The V3.2 plateau gate (committed in the May-2026 Samir-Stack re-derivation) was originally enforced AFTER the per-cell run finished — you'd compute every cell's full result, then check spread across them. This is operationally backwards: a 30%+ spread is a *design-time* failure mode, knowable from the WFO Sharpe alone (no MC paths, no noise sweeps required). Running MC + noise on a non-plateau design produces a more detailed verdict but does not change the conclusion — the design has no robust parameter region. E1b (VRP v2 audit, 2026-05-15) demonstrated this: the plateau pre-flight aborted in ~60 seconds, producing a STRONGER negative result than E1's full ~6-minute audit. The lesson cost almost nothing to learn precisely because the pre-flight ran first.

**How to apply.**

1. Pre-register a `PLATEAU_NEIGHBOURS` dict in every audit harness — 6 cells, each varying ONE parameter from the canonical by ±1 step. The choice of "step" is part of the pre-reg, not retroactive.
2. At audit start, after data load + sanctuary slice + causality smoke test, call `plateau_pre_flight(visible, folds, ...) -> (rel_spread, sharpes_dict)`.
3. If `rel_spread > 0.30`: write a minimal result log capturing the per-cell Sharpes + the failed gate, and return WITHOUT running the per-cell audit.
4. Implementation reference: `research/vrp_v2/run_vrp_v2_audit.py::plateau_pre_flight`.

**Source.** Pre-Reg E1b VRP Capture v2 Percentile Gates 2026-05-15.md, §3 + §4.1. Spread 44.66% over the 6-neighbour grid; full audit not run.

### L28 — Input-noise robustness (L24) and parameter-spread robustness (V3.2) are complementary, not redundant

**Rule.** The Varma noise-injection gate (L24) and the V3.2 plateau check are *different fragility tests*. L24 perturbs the **input price series** and re-runs the strategy at the same parameters — it catches strategies fragile to small data noise (e.g. bare-threshold gate near 1.05 flips under 0.1σ input noise). V3.2 perturbs the **parameter values** and re-runs the strategy on the same data — it catches strategies fragile to small parameter shifts (e.g. enter_q=0.55 vs 0.65 gives 45% different Sharpe). A strategy can pass one and fail the other.

**Why.** E1 (VRP v1, bare-threshold) failed L24 because the threshold value is fixed at 1.05 and any noise that pushes the signal across that line flips the regime decision. E1b (VRP v2, percentile-rolling gates) was DESIGNED to fix L24 — the gate threshold IS the rolling quantile, so 0.1σ input noise shifts the signal and the threshold roughly in tandem. But E1b failed V3.2: the choice of `window_d`, `enter_q`, `exit_q` parameter VALUES gives a 45% Sharpe spread across the neighbourhood. The strategy is robust to input noise but not to parameter choice. Both fragilities are deployment-blockers — you don't know which `enter_q` to ship.

**How to apply.**

1. Both gates run in every audit, against different perturbation axes.
2. The plateau pre-flight (L27) catches V3.2 failures cheaply and early; the L24 noise gate runs per cell as the 5th decision axis.
3. A strategy needs to pass BOTH to be deployment-eligible (in addition to the other 4 axes of the matrix).
4. Mitigations are different: L24 failure suggests redesigning the SIGNAL (e.g. percentile-rolling gate, sigmoid scaling, smoothing). V3.2 failure suggests the STRATEGY itself has no edge — small parameter changes shouldn't matter for a real signal.

**Source.** E1 + E1b combined evidence, 2026-05-15. Two different VRP designs each failed one of the two gates; the V3.2 failure (E1b) was the stronger negative result.

### L30 — Vol-target overlays demote the noise axis from `best` to `mid`

**Rule.** Adding a continuous vol-target overlay (`position *= min(1, target_vol / realised_vol_20d)`) to a strategy whose un-overlaid version passes the noise gate cleanly (`best`) will typically demote the overlaid version's noise axis to `mid` — passes mean-degradation gate, fails worst-case gate. Mechanism: the realised-vol estimator in the denominator is itself noise-sensitive on short windows, so small input perturbations produce LARGE position swings on the bars where the vol estimate is most noise-perturbed. In expectation this washes out (mean Sharpe degradation < 30%), but in the tail of trials it does not (5th-percentile Sharpe degradation > 30%).

**Why.** GEM J3 5-axis re-audit, 2026-05-15: cells with `ann_vol_target=None` (C1, C3, C4, C5, C7 — the un-overlaid Antonacci variants) all have noise axis = `best`. Cells with `ann_vol_target=0.10` (C8, C9, C12, C14, C15 — the vol-target-overlaid variants) all have noise axis = `mid`. The vol-target arithmetic is what flips the axis class. C13 (`ann_vol_target=0.20`, twice the target) demotes further to noise axis = `worst` — confirming the mechanism is the overlay's noise amplification, not a binary on/off effect.

**How to apply.**

1. **Expect the demotion.** When adding a vol-target overlay, predict the noise axis will go from `best` to `mid` on the strategy's verdict line. Plan for CONDITIONAL_WATCHPOINT, not DEPLOY, unless one of the mitigations below is also applied.
2. **Mitigation A: smooth the vol estimator.** Replace `realised_vol_20d` with an EWMA over a longer effective horizon (e.g. half-life 40 bars) or a longer rolling window (60d). Reduces noise on the denominator that drives position-swing variance.
3. **Mitigation B: cap per-bar position-change rate.** `|w[t] - w[t-1]| <= 0.05` per day. Decouples the position trajectory from the vol-estimator trajectory — the overlay can't move the position faster than the cap allows, so input noise can't propagate into large weight swings.
4. **Mitigation C: percentile-target instead of fixed-value target.** Replace `target_vol = 0.10` with `target_vol = rolling_percentile(realised_vol, window=252, q=0.40)`. Same L26 mitigation pattern as for regime gates. The target moves with the data so input noise affects both numerator and denominator.
5. **Accept the demotion.** If the strategy's other 4 axes are strong and the noise axis is `mid` (not `worst`), CONDITIONAL_WATCHPOINT is an acceptable verdict for already-deployed strategies — pair with monitoring and a future redesign PR. (This is the GEM C12 path.)

**Source.** GEM J3 5-axis re-audit 2026-05-15 (Pre-Reg J3 Noise Robustness 5th Axis 2026-05-15.md §4.2). Five vol-target cells uniformly demoted; five non-overlaid cells uniformly retained `best`.

### L31 — EWMA vol estimator is the preferred mitigation for noise-fragile vol-target overlays

**Rule.** Of the three L30 mitigations tested empirically in J4 (EWMA vol estimator, per-bar Δw cap, rolling-percentile vol target), **EWMA wins on every axis that matters for the CROSS_ASSET_MOMENTUM class:** it recovers the noise axis from `mid` to `best` while preserving point Sharpe within ~3% of the baseline. Per-bar Δw cap also recovers the noise axis but at 20–30% Sharpe drag. Rolling-quantile vol target makes the noise axis WORSE (mid → worst). Default to EWMA. Reach for Δw cap only when an operational hard-cap constraint is required for non-noise reasons (e.g. risk-budget). Do NOT use rolling-quantile vol targets.

**Why.** GEM J4 audit, 2026-05-15: three DEPLOY winners (A1 EWMA hl=40, B1 Δw cap 5%, B2 Δw cap 10%) and two FAILED variants (C1 quantile q=0.40, C2 quantile q=0.50). Ranking by CI_lo (J3 §3 tie-breaker): A1 (+0.387) ≫ B2 (+0.270) > B1 (+0.192). The Δw cap mechanically slows the strategy's response to genuine regime changes too — recovering noise robustness at the cost of edge. EWMA smooths only the vol estimator, which is the noise-source itself; it's the surgical fix.

**How to apply.**

1. Whenever `ann_vol_target` is set on a `CROSS_ASSET_MOMENTUM`-class strategy, **default to `vol_estimator_kind = "ewma"`, `vol_estimator_halflife = 40`**. Document the choice in the pre-reg's §3.
2. If the strategy's pre-J3 audit had noise axis = `best` without an overlay AND the post-J3 audit has noise axis = `mid` because of the overlay, the canonical fix is L31's EWMA path — try it BEFORE redesigning the strategy logic.
3. Sharpe trade-off: expect ~2–5% point-Sharpe drag from EWMA vs rolling-std on the same window. CI_lo typically tightens (CI is smaller because EWMA reduces the strategy's tail-trial variance). If CI_lo crosses zero, the underlying signal is too weak — the EWMA isn't masking the problem, it's revealing it.
4. **Do not combine with rolling-quantile vol target.** L32 explains the mechanism.

**Source.** Pre-Reg J4 GEM Noise-Robust Redesign 2026-05-15.md §4. Three winners by CI_lo: A1_ewma_hl40 (+0.387), B2_cap_10pct (+0.270), B1_cap_5pct (+0.192). Baseline (C12 reproduced as C0): Sharpe +0.8016, noise axis `mid`. A1: Sharpe +0.7773, noise axis `best`. ~3% Sharpe drag bought DEPLOY-eligibility.

### L32 — L26's percentile-gating pattern does NOT transfer to denominator-style overlays

**Rule.** The L26 mitigation (replace a fixed threshold `s >= K` with a percentile-rolling threshold `s >= rolling_quantile(s, w, q)`) was designed for THRESHOLD signals where the noise unilaterally crosses the gate. It does **not** generalise to DENOMINATOR-style scaling overlays where the noisy quantity is divided by another noisy quantity (e.g. vol target scaling `target / realised_vol`). Replacing the fixed numerator with a rolling-quantile of the denominator produces phase-lagged noise on both sides and AMPLIFIES position swings rather than damping them. The strategy moves from noise=mid to noise=worst.

**Why.** GEM J4 audit C1_qtile_q40 (vol_target = 252-day rolling 40th-pctile of realised vol) and C2_qtile_q50 (50th-pctile) both demoted from C0's noise=mid to noise=worst. Mechanism: when the realised-vol estimator gets perturbed by an input-noise injection on close prices, BOTH the numerator (the rolling quantile) AND the denominator (the current realised vol) move — but with phase lag (the quantile is a rolling statistic of the historical denominator). The position-scaling ratio (target / realised) varies MORE under perturbation than with a fixed target, because the target is now a noise-derived statistic too.

**How to apply.**

1. When a strategy's noise fragility is due to a SCALING DENOMINATOR (vol target, sizing kernel, leverage normaliser), reach for L31's mitigation set (EWMA estimator, Δw cap) — NOT for L26's percentile pattern.
2. The two patterns cover disjoint regions of the design space. L26 is for THRESHOLD/GATE noise. L31 is for SCALE/DENOMINATOR noise. Don't cross-apply.
3. If you find yourself reaching for "use a rolling quantile to make the target adaptive", check whether the noise-source is the target's value (L26 territory) or the noisy denominator it's dividing (L31 territory). Only the former is L26-compatible.

**Source.** Pre-Reg J4 GEM Noise-Robust Redesign 2026-05-15.md §4.4 (mitigation C arm). Both C1 (q=0.40) and C2 (q=0.50) demoted from noise=mid (baseline) to noise=worst. Sharpe unchanged within bootstrap noise; the harm was entirely on the noise-robustness axis.

### L33 — Session-bounded strategies have asymmetric tail risk on regime-shuffled MC

**Rule.** A strategy whose alpha lives in a SHORT FRACTION of the trading day (overnight-only, intraday-only, event-window-only) will produce a Rel-MC ratio > 1.0 — i.e. STRATEGY MaxDD HIGHER than buy-hold — under block-bootstrap regime-shuffled MC paths, even when the strategy's full-sample MaxDD looks lower than buy-hold. Mechanism: the strategy captures the bad moves IN its session (e.g. overnight gaps) but does not participate in the recovery OUTSIDE its session (e.g. intraday rallies). On MC paths, the bad-session bar can land on a path where the recovery never occurred, leaving the strategy stranded at the gap-down level. Full-sample MaxDD masks this because the empirical sequence happens to contain both legs in proximity; MC paths break the co-occurrence.

**Why.** G4 overnight-only audit, 2026-05-15: every cost-bearing cell produced a relative MC ratio in 1.27-2.54 vs the 0.80 deployment gate. The published literature (LPS 2019) reports lower drawdowns on the empirical sequence; our MC says the lower drawdown is path-dependent, not a structural property. C5 buy-hold has Rel-MC ratio = 1.0 by definition (compared against itself). C2 (overnight + intraday-short) has the worst Rel-MC at 2.54 — the short-intraday leg adds tail risk during recovery sessions.

**How to apply.**

1. **Predict the Rel-MC failure** before running the audit on any session-bounded design. Don't budget DEPLOY probability on the Rel-MC axis for these strategies.
2. **Design choices that mitigate L33:** (a) extend the holding window to bridge into the recovery session; (b) add an explicit hedge during the off-session window (e.g. short VIX in the intraday window if overnight-long); (c) accept the asymmetric tail and require an ALPHA-on-COST ratio that justifies it (typically only at futures-level costs, not ETF-level).
3. **Don't override the Rel-MC gate** to admit a session-bounded strategy under the L17 logic. The L17 gate is correctly calibrated; the L33 strategy class has a true tail-risk problem that L17 is detecting.
4. **Cross-asset version recommended** — LPS' actual canonical setup is cross-sectional (long overnight winners, short overnight losers across S&P500), which is market-neutral and partially immunises against the asymmetric whole-market tail. Single-asset overnight-only on SPY (G4 v1) is the wrong specification.

**Source.** G4 overnight session decomposition audit, 2026-05-15. C1 overnight-only (cost-bearing) Rel-MC ratio = 2.22; C3 (no costs) Rel-MC ratio = 0.00 — confirming the issue is the asymmetric tail produced by sessions-bounded exposure, NOT the cost structure (which produces a different failure mode visible on CI_lo).

### L34 — Databento GLBX.MDP3 continuous `.c.1` queries are pathologically slow

**Rule.** Databento's continuous-contract symbology on `GLBX.MDP3` materialises `.c.0` (front-month) bars quickly (~0.005 s/bar) but `.c.N` for N>0 falls off a cliff to ~5 s/bar — a 1000× slowdown for the same `ohlcv-1d` schema. **How to apply.**

### L35 — A correlated proxy is NOT a substitute for a structural signal — directional behavior must be re-checked in the target sample

**Rule.** When the data source for a strategy's intended signal becomes unavailable and you reach for a "proxy" signal that the literature reports as highly correlated with the original, you have NOT preserved the strategy. You have created a DIFFERENT STRATEGY HYPOTHESIS whose directional behavior in your specific sample may be opposite to the original's. A reported cross-paper correlation of 0.65 leaves 58% of the variance unexplained — and the unexplained component can flip the sign of expected return in cross-sectional sorts. Pre-register the proxy as its own hypothesis and don't conflate its result with the original.

**Why.** D2 was pre-registered as commodity futures carry using the strict log(M1/M2) basis signal (BGR 2019). When M2 data became unavailable, the audit pivoted to BGR §3.2's documented proxy: 12-month rolling yield on M1. BGR reports ρ=0.65 between the two. In our 2000-2026 sample, the rolling-yield strategy produced ALL cells with Sharpe -0.30 to -0.62 (uniformly negative — the strategy LOSES money). The 5-cell plateau pre-flight spread was 109.81%. The rolling-yield proxy is mathematically a cross-sectional 12-month momentum signal on commodities; in our sample, commodities exhibit medium-horizon mean reversion against this signal. Whether strict-carry M1/M2 (the original BGR signal) ALSO loses money in this sample is unknown and untestable until M2 data is available.

**How to apply.**

1. **Never substitute a "proxy" signal mid-audit.** Either acquire the right data or pre-register the proxy as a SEPARATE STRATEGY HYPOTHESIS (e.g. "D2b: cross-sectional 12-month commodity momentum").
2. **Pre-test directional behavior** before committing the audit to the proxy. Cheap sniff-test: correlation between the proxy and a small validation slice of the original signal in the user's specific sample. If they have opposite signs in any meaningful sub-period, treat the proxy as a different hypothesis.
3. **Document the unsubstituted-original.** D2 retired the rolling-yield variant; the strict-carry hypothesis is OPEN, awaiting M2 data. Don't let the proxy's failure poison the original.
4. **Generalisation.** Correlation is NOT enough — sign agreement on the relevant moments (cross-sectional rank, time-series sign, regime conditional) is the lower bar.

**Source.** D2 Commodity Futures Carry audit 2026-05-15. Strict BGR carry: pre-registered, blocked on data. Rolling-yield proxy: pre-flight FAILED with negative Sharpes across all 5 plateau cells (-0.30 to -0.62), 109.81% spread, audit aborted per L27. RETIRED as a separate hypothesis; the strict BGR carry remains open pending M2 data.

### L36 — Survivorship bias + post-2009 momentum decay flip the sign of cross-sectional equity momentum on current-constituent samples

**Rule.** A cross-sectional momentum strategy on a CURRENT-constituent stock universe (e.g. "today's S&P 500", scraped from Wikipedia/yfinance) will systematically underperform the published academic numbers — often producing a negative Sharpe where the literature reports positive — because two effects compound: (a) the universe is selected for past outperformance (stocks join the index AFTER they win), so the historical "winners" in the cross-section are exactly the names whose subsequent returns mean-revert; (b) the cross-sectional 12-month skip-1 momentum factor on US large-cap equities has decayed/inverted since 2009 (multiple papers: Daniel-Moskowitz 2016, Asness-Frazzini-Israel-Moskowitz 2014, Stivers-Sun 2016). The two effects are NOT orthogonal — the bias's victims are exactly the stocks the strategy shorts, at peaks.

**Why.** A1 Residual Momentum audit (2026-05-15) on 496 current-S&P-500 stocks over 2000-2026: plateau pre-flight produced uniformly negative Sharpe (-0.25 to -0.42) across 5 grid cells (canonical window=12 skip=1, ±1-step neighbours on window and skip). Relative Sharpe spread = 66.44% (gate 30%) — parameter-fragile on top of negative expected sign. The BHM 2011 published Sharpe was on survivorship-free CRSP/Compustat data through ~2008. Reproducing this on retail-accessible yfinance current-constituents in a sample dominated by post-decay years inverts the sign.

**How to apply.**

1. **Discount.** Cross-sectional equity momentum on a current-S&P-500 universe should be discounted by 0.2-0.4 Sharpe vs published academic numbers as a baseline expectation.
2. **Data fix.** A faithful BHM-style audit requires survivorship-free historical constituent lists + delisted-stock prices (CRSP/Compustat via WRDS, ~$2k/yr). Pre-register the data dependency explicitly; without it, the audit measures something different and shouldn't quote the published numbers as the expected baseline.
3. **Residualization does not fix the bias.** Stripping FF3 exposure removes the systematic-factor component but preserves the SIGN of the cross-sectional rank. If raw momentum is negative on the biased universe, residualized momentum is also negative (residual ≈ raw minus factor projections).
4. **Pivot options.** Alternative momentum strategies that survive: (i) time-series momentum on commodity / FX futures (TSMOM — see B4); (ii) cross-sectional on small-caps where the post-2009 decay was less pronounced; (iii) cross-sectional on international developed (MSCI EAFE) — each its own pre-reg.

**Source.** A1 Residual Momentum audit 2026-05-15. 496 stocks × 6,348 visible bars × 5 plateau cells; all cells produced negative Sharpe; audit aborted at plateau gate per L27. Full result log in `directives/Pre-Reg A1 Residual Momentum 2026-05-15.md` §4.

### L37 — Cross-sectional and time-series momentum have decoupled regime profiles post-2009

**Rule.** Cross-sectional momentum (long winners / short losers within a universe) and time-series momentum (long if the asset's own past return is positive, short if negative) USED to track each other in 1990-2008 academic samples. They have decoupled post-2009: cross-sectional has decayed/inverted on US large-cap equities (L36); time-series has persisted on commodity and FX futures (Asness-Moskowitz-Pedersen 2013 and replications through 2024). A failed audit on one does NOT predict a failed audit on the other.

**How to apply.**

1. Treat each momentum-family strategy on the backlog as INDEPENDENT. A1's failure does not preclude B4 (TSMOM on commodity / FX futures) on the backlog or B2 (Carver EWMAC ensemble).
2. Cross-sectional momentum needs a different vehicle (small-caps, EM, or a single-stock cross-section that's not survivorship-biased) to be testable post-A1.
3. The decoupling is itself a research finding worth documenting in any future cross-asset / cross-strategy survey — XSMom and TSMOM are no longer interchangeable proxies.

**Source.** A1 Residual Momentum 2026-05-15 (negative XSMom) vs the backlog item B4 (TSMOM) which is still untested and has different supporting literature.

### L38 — Falsification audits should test the obvious data-construction first

**Rule.** When an audit produces an unexpected negative result (or any verdict that disagrees with published academic findings), the first thing to re-check is the data construction — not the strategy code. Specifically: total-return vs price-only, currency conversion, splits, dividends, survivorship, look-ahead on universe membership. The strategy code can be unit-tested on synthetic data (and probably already is); the data is the part the framework cannot prove correct from inside. The order of operations: synthetic-test confirms code → data-audit confirms inputs → only THEN trust the live-data verdict.

**Why.** A1 v1 produced uniformly negative Sharpe (-0.25 to -0.42) on residual momentum. The honest self-audit asked "could there be a data bug?" — and found one: the download script dropped yfinance's `adj_close` (total return) and kept `close` (price-only), under-ranking dividend stocks in the cross-section. Re-downloading with total returns and re-running the audit produced cells -0.28 to -0.44 — slightly more negative, NOT more positive. The data fix CONFIRMED the underlying L36 lesson rather than rescuing it. But: had the fix flipped the sign, we'd have caught a false retirement and saved the lesson catalogue from a wrong attribution. The 15 min of "wasted" data-fix work is the insurance policy that makes negative results trustworthy.

**How to apply.**

1. For every "RETIRE" / "SUSPECT" verdict from a fresh audit, write 2-3 hypotheses about data-construction errors that could produce a false negative.
2. Pick the cheapest one to test (typically: data-source columns, currency, time-zone). Patch + rerun.
3. If the verdict moves toward positive, the audit was contaminated. Re-pre-register and re-audit.
4. If the verdict stays negative (or gets more negative), the original lesson is empirically confirmed and now has TWO independent signal vectors (v1 + v2).
5. The work is bounded: don't run more than ~2-3 data-construction fixes before accepting the verdict. Diminishing returns set in fast.

**Source.** A1 v1 (price-only) vs A1 v2 (total return), 2026-05-15. Both negative; L36 confirmed via post-mortem cross-check.

### L39 — TSMOM persistence is qualitative, not quantitative, on narrow single-asset-class universes

**Rule.** The L37 prediction that time-series momentum has persisted post-2009 on futures is **qualitatively** confirmed (sign of returns is positive on canonical settings) — but the magnitude on a single-asset-class universe (e.g. commodities only) is far below the published academic numbers from multi-asset baskets. 1. **Set the success threshold appropriately.** On a single-asset-class universe at retail data resolution, plan for Sharpe in [0.15, 0.5] range; >0.5 is suspicious and requires extra validation. >0.8 is implausible.
2. **For a faithful MOP replication, plan a multi-asset-class universe.** Need ~50 instruments spanning commodities + FX + equity indices + bonds. Each class is its own data-acquisition project.
3. **Don't quote MOP 2012's ~1.0 Sharpe as a baseline expectation** for retail-implementable single-asset-class TSMOM. The published number is a multi-class diversification benefit, not a per-asset alpha.

**Source.** B4 audit 2026-05-15 on 24-commodity yfinance universe over 2000-2026. Canonical cell: +0.16 Sharpe; plateau spread 360% (range crosses zero); aborted per L27.

### L40 — yfinance continuous-future prices may contaminate multi-month-trailing-return signals via opaque roll logic

2. **yfinance is acceptable for shorter-horizon strategies** (intraday, weekly, sub-3-month trailing) where roll happens infrequently relative to the signal lookback.
3. **Document the data construction in the pre-reg.** When the strategy's signal horizon spans roll dates, the data-source choice is a strategic decision, not an implementation detail.

### L42 — Stitched-vs-yfinance correlation target is 0.85-0.92, NOT 0.95+

**Rule.** When validating a roll-stitched continuous-futures series against yfinance's `=F` continuous symbols, the EXPECTED Pearson correlation of daily log returns is 0.85-0.92 over a multi-year window. Correlation > 0.95 indicates the stitch is inheriting yfinance's roll-contamination (Yahoo's continuous prices are unadjusted, so they have artificial 5-15% single-day jumps on roll boundaries). Correlation < 0.80 indicates a stitching bug (wrong contract picked on a given date, or back-adjustment ratio applied to the wrong block).

**Why.** D2b 2026-05-15 §4.2: CL roll-stitched M1 vs yfinance CL=F over 746 common business days gave rho = 0.879. Top-1% disagreement days clustered on yfinance's roll boundaries where yfinance showed ±15-32% single-day moves and our back-adjusted series showed ±5-8% moves (real CL volatility, no roll jump). Filtering out the top-5% disagreement days raised rho to 0.954. The 0.879 number reflects the cleanup, not a bug.

**How to apply.**

1. **L38 abort gate (rho < 0.80) remains valid** — that level of disagreement still indicates something is structurally wrong.
2. **Drop the 0.95 PASS target.** Replace with: `0.85 <= rho <= 0.93` is the target; `0.80 <= rho < 0.85` is acceptable with documentation; `rho >= 0.95` is suspicious (audit the stitch for inherited roll-contamination); `rho < 0.80` aborts.
3. **Always inspect the top-5% disagreement days.** If they cluster on roll boundaries AND yfinance shows the larger move, the stitch is doing its job. If they cluster on non-roll days OR your stitched shows the larger move, something is wrong.
4. **Compare distribution moments**, not just correlation. Stitched should have lower max |daily return| and lower std than yfinance for the same period if the cleanup is working.

**Source.** D2b CL stitched-vs-yfinance validation 2026-05-15. See `scripts/validate_stitched_vs_yfinance.py`.

### L43 — A high canonical Sharpe with knife-edge plateau dropoff is NOT promotable, even if the falsification hypothesis is supported

**Rule.** When a canonical cell produces a strong Sharpe (e.g. +1.6) but a close-neighbour cell in the pre-registered plateau bucket drops materially (e.g. +0.4 at lookback=9 months vs +1.6 at lookback=12 months), the strategy is plateau-failing per L27. **This holds even if the audit's separate falsification hypothesis (e.g. "L40 was load-bearing") is empirically supported.** The plateau gate is about robustness to small parameter perturbations; falsification hypotheses are about whether a research conjecture is correct. These are independent. A plateau-failing strategy with a confirmed hypothesis tells you the *thesis* is right but the *parameterisation* is brittle.

**Why.** B4b audit 2026-05-15: TSMOM on IBKR roll-stitched M1 data produced C1 canonical Sharpe = +1.6309 vs B4 (yfinance M1) = +0.16. The 10x lift confirmed L40 (yfinance roll-contamination was load-bearing on the magnitude). BUT P_window_9 (a plateau neighbour) collapsed to +0.45 while P_window_12, P_window_15, and P_skip_2 all clustered at +1.4 to +1.6. Relative spread 72.48% >> 30% L27 gate. The audit RETIRED with H1 SUPPORTED — a counterintuitive but methodologically correct outcome.

**How to apply.**

1. **Plateau-fail aborts the audit, full stop.** Per L27. Do not write a "but the canonical is fine" loophole.
2. **Record the falsification verdict separately.** The result log should have §H1 (hypothesis verdict) AND §verdict (deployment verdict). They can disagree.
3. **The honest follow-up is a parameter-ensemble pre-reg.** If C1=+1.6, P_window_9=+0.45, P_window_15=+1.55, the natural next step is to pre-register a "9/12/15-month blend" cell (`weights = mean of sign(ret_9), sign(ret_12), sign(ret_15)`). This neutralises window-knife-edge risk and is testable on the same data.
4. **Do NOT widen the plateau bucket to exclude the bad neighbour.** That's post-hoc parameter fitting (anti-pattern A4).

**Source.** B4b TSMOM audit 2026-05-15 on IBKR roll-stitched M1.

### L44 — Carry / basis signals must use RAW (non-back-adjusted) M1 and M2; back-adjusted continuous series cannot be ratioed cross-contract

**Rule.** A continuous-futures series that has been back-adjusted by ratio at each roll carries a cumulative multiplicative factor `adj(t) = product over roll dates of (new_contract / old_contract)`. This factor is independent for each constructed series (M1 has its own roll-ratios, M2 has different ones). Therefore:

```text
log(stitched_M1[t] / stitched_M2[t]) = log(F_M1_raw[t] / F_M2_raw[t]) + log(adj_M1(t) / adj_M2(t))
                                                                        ^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^
                                                                        step-function bias per commodity
```

The bias term is commodity-specific (depends on each commodity's roll history) and corrupts the **cross-sectional ranking** that carry strategies rely on. Using back-adjusted M1 and M2 to compute the strict basis is a silent data-construction bug — the signal will be SYSTEMATICALLY biased, not just noisy.

**Why.** D2b audit 2026-05-15 first run produced C1 canonical Sharpe = **-1.6273** and all 5 plateau cells strongly negative (-0.93 to -3.20). On critical review prompted by the user ("verify it — think critically from scratch to make sure all aligns"), inspection showed the CLJ5/CLH5 raw forward-curve ratio on 2025-01-15 = 0.9809 (1.9% backwardation), but the stitched M2/M1 ratio = 0.9332 (looks like 7% backwardation — false signal). The bias compounds across rolls and varies per commodity. After fix (raw stitched variants used for signal, back-adjusted for returns), corrected C1 = +0.0847 — sign and magnitude both materially different. The audit still RETIRED at plateau pre-flight, but on a noise-around-zero result, not a strongly-negative one. The conclusion (strict carry not deployable on this 3y window) is the same; the interpretation (proxy IS load-bearing in pulling proxy result strongly negative vs strict's near-zero) was different.

**How to apply.**

1. **For ANY cross-contract or curve-shape signal** (basis, contango/backwardation, calendar spreads, term-structure slope): use RAW close of the currently-active contract per date. `research.futures_stitching.build_continuous(..., back_adjust=False)` and `_raw_stitched_D.parquet` files are the canonical inputs.
2. **For ANY holding-period return calculation** (TSMOM cumulative, daily MTM on a held position, equity curve): use BACK-ADJUSTED continuous series. The back-adjustment by ratio is precisely what makes daily log-returns reflect the actual P&L of holding the contract through rolls.
3. **`carry_returns()` now accepts `M1_signal_df` / `M2_signal_df`** overrides for this exact reason. Pass raw series for signal, adjusted for returns.
4. **Sanity check at code review** (anti-anti-pattern): for any strategy whose signal is `log(M1/M2)` or `(M1 - M2)/M2` on continuous-futures data, demand evidence the developer used raw front/second-month — not the back-adjusted series.
5. **Regression tests** are in `tests/test_futures_stitching.py`:
   - `test_raw_mode_returns_unadjusted_close`
   - `test_raw_mode_has_jumps_at_rolls_while_adjusted_does_not`
   - `test_raw_ratio_is_true_basis_adjusted_ratio_is_biased`

**Source.** D2b audit 2026-05-15 + user-requested critical review. See `research/futures_stitching/stitch.py` (`back_adjust` flag), `research/futures_carry/carry_strategy.py` (`M1_signal_df` override), and the regression tests above.

### L45 — Plateau-mitigation ensembles are partial fixes; report mitigation pct, but abort if still > L27 gate

**Rule.** When a follow-up audit tests an ensemble (or any other mitigation) against a plateau-failing baseline audit, two distinct verdicts must be reported and they can disagree:

1. **L27 plateau gate** — relative spread ≤ 30%? PASS or FAIL.
2. **Mitigation effectiveness** — percentage reduction in plateau spread vs the baseline.

If mitigation is material (≥ 30% reduction) but the gate still fails, the result is **research-alive, deployment-dead**: the hypothesis (ensembling mitigates) is partly confirmed, but the strategy is still not promotable. Do NOT relax the L27 gate to make the mitigation "pass". Instead, log:

- The baseline's plateau spread.
- The mitigation's plateau spread.
- The mitigation percentage.
- The remaining-fragility analysis (which neighbour cells still pull the spread? what's the regime fingerprint?).

Future audits build on this. The mitigation lesson can compound across audits (e.g., L45 + a regime-overlay can deliver what L45 alone cannot).

**Why.** B4c audit 2026-05-15 tested L43's mitigation claim: that ensembling 3 nearby lookback windows (9, 12, 15) dissolves B4b's plateau knife-edge (P_window_9 = +0.45 vs C1_canonical = +1.63, spread 72.48%). Result: ensemble C1 = +1.43 with plateau spread **47.93%** — H1 PARTIALLY SUPPORTED (33.9% mitigation), H2 SUPPORTED (87.9% Sharpe preservation), but L27 30% gate STILL FAILS. The remaining fragility is concentrated at short-window neighbours (P_shift_short = (6,9,12) Sharpe +0.88 vs P_drop_short = (12,15) Sharpe +1.69), suggesting the 2023-2025 regime structurally disfavours 6-9-month momentum on this universe.

**How to apply.**

1. **Always benchmark mitigation audits against their baseline.** The result log §4 must include baseline plateau spread, mitigation plateau spread, AND mitigation percentage. Do not just report the new audit's spread in isolation.
2. **Mitigation ≥ 30% spread-reduction WITHOUT L27 pass = "research alive, deployment dead".** Document and propose the next escalation (e.g., B4c → B2 Carver EWMAC adds many more speeds and an explicit signal cap).
3. **Mitigation < 30% spread-reduction = baseline-equivalent failure.** Do not pursue further variants of the same approach; pivot to a different mitigation class.
4. **Never relax the L27 gate to accommodate a partial-mitigation cell.** The gate exists to prevent parameter overfit; reducing it would defeat its purpose.
5. **Remaining-fragility analysis is gold.** Report which neighbour cells still pull the spread — this tells you what the next mitigation must address.

**Source.** B4c audit 2026-05-15 (`directives/Pre-Reg B4c Window-Ensemble TSMOM 2026-05-15.md` §4.3 + §4.5). See also `research/tsmom/run_b4c_audit.py` which structures the result log around the mitigation-percentage report.

### L46 — Decision-matrix verdict and the binding constraint must be reported separately; the binding constraint wins

**Rule.** The 5-axis decision matrix (L24) and the bootstrap CI gate (V3.6 research-math: "A strategy whose 95% lower bound is ≤ 0 is `tier=unconfirmed`") are INDEPENDENT promotion gates. They can return DIFFERENT verdicts on the same audit. When this happens, the **strictest constraint binds**, and the audit result log must report BOTH verdicts explicitly along with which constraint is binding. Do NOT collapse them into a single verdict; that hides important diagnostic information about WHAT is blocking deployment.

This matters most when the binding constraint is **sample size**, not signal quality. A negative CI_lo on a high-Sharpe strategy with 2 WFO folds tells you "the signal is real, but the CI is too wide to certify it at 95% confidence with this much data." The fix is "wait for more data" or "expand the universe to tighten CI", NOT "redesign the strategy" or "loosen the gate".

**Why.** B2 EWMAC audit 2026-05-15: C1_canonical (3-speed EWMAC ensemble) produced Sharpe = +2.0218, plateau spread = 15.28% (passes L27 cleanly), MC = 0.0 P(drawdown > 35%), DSR ≈ 1.0, noise axis = "best" (passes both mean and worst). The 5-axis decision matrix returned **CONDITIONAL_WATCHPOINT**. But CI_lo = **-0.044** (95% bootstrap lower bound just below zero, driven by only 2 WFO folds × ~120 OOS days each). The V3.6 research-math CI gate independently requires CI_lo > 0 for deployment-tier; with negative CI_lo, the strategy is **tier_unconfirmed**.

Without L46, an audit log might report just CONDITIONAL_WATCHPOINT and confuse a reader into thinking deployment is appropriate. With L46, the log reports BOTH verdicts and flags the negative CI_lo as the binding constraint, plus the regime-aware fix ("wait 12 months for more data + re-audit").

**How to apply.**

1. **Every result-log §4.7 (or equivalent promotion section)** must show both the 5-axis verdict and the bootstrap-CI verdict, separately. If the decision-matrix verdict is DEPLOY or CONDITIONAL_WATCHPOINT but CI_lo ≤ 0, write: "Decision matrix: X. Bootstrap CI gate: tier_unconfirmed (CI_lo = Y ≤ 0). **Binding constraint: bootstrap CI.**"
2. **Cite the binding constraint, not the verdict.** "Not promoted: bootstrap CI_lo = -0.044 on 2-fold WFO" is correct. "Not promoted: CONDITIONAL_WATCHPOINT" is misleading (the verdict was *eligible* for promotion under the matrix rule).
3. **Diagnose CI-bound vs signal-bound.** If the strategy has Sharpe > +1.5 but CI_lo < 0, it's CI-bound (sample size). If Sharpe < +0.5 AND CI_lo < 0, it's signal-bound (real edge weak). The remediation differs.
4. **Sample-size-bound results are NOT failures.** They mean "the signal exists, the sample is too short to certify it." The follow-up actions are: shadow-deployment, universe expansion, longer history (paid data), or meta-labeler — NOT strategy redesign.
5. **Don't loosen either gate.** The L27 plateau gate and the CI gate both exist for valid reasons; relaxing either to fit a specific result is the parameter-overfit anti-pattern.

**Source.** B2 EWMAC audit 2026-05-15 (`directives/Pre-Reg B2 Carver EWMAC Ensemble 2026-05-15.md` §4.7). The result log explicitly separates the matrix verdict, the CI gate verdict, and the binding constraint, and proposes shadow-deployment + universe expansion as the right remediation rather than strategy redesign.

### L48 — A high Sharpe on a narrow universe + narrow window must be re-validated on a broader universe + window BEFORE deployment promotion; sample-size CI tightening is necessary but NOT sufficient

**Rule.** When an audit produces a high canonical Sharpe (e.g., +2.0) on a narrow window (e.g., 2-3y) with a small WFO fold count (e.g., 2 folds), and the audit is blocked from deployment by the bootstrap CI gate (CI_lo near zero), the diagnosis must include both:

1. **Sample-size hypothesis (L46)** — "the signal is real; the CI is wide because there's not enough data". Remediation: shadow-deploy + wait, OR universe-expand.
2. **Regime-artifact hypothesis (L48)** — "the strategy edge is real ONLY in this specific window; the narrow universe is concealing the regime-dependence". Remediation: re-audit on a BROADER and LONGER sample to falsify the regime hypothesis.

**These are independent and BOTH must be tested before promotion.** Universe expansion + longer history simultaneously addresses BOTH (more cross-section AND more time). If the broader audit reverses the sign of the canonical Sharpe (positive → negative), L48 is confirmed and the strategy is RETIRED with a research note that "the 2023-2025 commodity sub-regime DID show positive Sharpe but the universal-trend hypothesis is rejected".

**Why.** B2 EWMAC audit 2026-05-15 (24-commodity IBKR 3y window, single-session: 2023-05 → 2026-05) produced **Sharpe +2.0218 with plateau spread 15.28%**, blocked from deployment only by CI_lo = -0.044 (2 WFO folds → wide CI). Carver-style universal-trend on multi-asset 21y data is negative-Sharpe in the post-2008 era (well-documented decline of pure trend strategies; what we recovered is the academic consensus).

**How to apply.**

1. **The "sample-size CI bottleneck" framing (L46) is necessary but insufficient.** Always pair it with a regime-falsification audit on a longer/broader sample BEFORE recommending shadow-deploy.
2. **A sign-reversal on broader data is decisive evidence of regime-artifact.** Plateau passing on a narrow universe + failing on a broader one is sufficient to RETIRE.

### L49 — Trend-of-trend gates rescue narrow plateau (artifacts) but not Sharpe; if the regime is per-asset, broad-index gates are model-mis-specified

**Rule.** When a trend-following strategy fails plateau on broad data (B2b: 37.10% spread), the natural mitigation is a trend-of-trend regime filter (B2c). The audit will likely show:

1. **Plateau spread shrinks dramatically** — often below the L27 30% gate — but this is largely a degenerate-cells artifact. With `absolute_trend` mode and zero deadband, the broad-trend gate is essentially constant (=1) most of the time, so most cells reproduce the no-filter baseline (B2b). Plateau "passing" under these conditions is NOT evidence the strategy is rescued; it's evidence the filter isn't doing anything.
2. **Sharpe doesn't materially improve** under absolute-trend gating. The B2c canonical (`fast=64, slow=256, mode=absolute_trend, deadband=0`) matched the B2b baseline (-0.2817) exactly.
3. **`directional` mode** (gate flips per-asset signal sign on negative broad-trend) can flip the Sharpe sign (B2c C2: -0.28 → +0.27). But this fails noise robustness and CI gates → still RETIRE. More importantly, the sign-flip improvement is diagnostic: **the regime is per-asset, not universe-wide**. A broad-index gate is the wrong granularity.

**Why.** B2c audit 2026-05-16 (31-instrument yfinance, 60 WFO folds): plateau spread collapsed to **3.40%** (cells degenerate to baseline because the broad-trend gate is near-constant); canonical C1 Sharpe = -0.2817 (identical to B2b baseline); C5 deadband=5 reduced to -0.2520 (tiny improvement); C2 directional flipped to +0.2710 (interesting but fails noise+CI gates). H1 trivially SUPPORTED, H2/H3/H4 all REJECTED. The C2 directional result is the methodological win: it diagnoses that the regime structure is per-instrument, justifying the I1 HMM escalation.

**How to apply.**

1. **A passing plateau check on a filter-gated audit is suspect.** Check whether the cells DEGENERATE (most cells producing identical Sharpe = the baseline). If so, the filter isn't doing anything; the plateau "pass" is a degeneracy artifact.
2. **Test multiple modes.** Absolute-trend gate (on/off) tests whether trading at all helps. Directional gate (sign-flip) tests whether the broad-trend SIGN aligns with per-asset trend directions. They probe different hypotheses.
3. **A Sharpe-sign flip on directional gate is diagnostic of per-asset regime structure.** If `directional` outperforms `absolute_trend` AND outperforms baseline, the per-asset trend signs are systematically WRONG when broad-trend is negative. That's information — the next mitigation should detect per-asset regime, not universe-wide.
4. **The right escalation after L49 is HMM-per-asset, not a smarter broad-index gate.** Don't waste cycles on finer broad-index parameters; move to per-asset regime detection (I1).

### L50 — Viterbi decoding is forward-backward and therefore NON-CAUSAL; for online/live trading use forward-only filtering instead

**Rule.** The Viterbi algorithm (`hmmlearn.GaussianHMM.predict()` and `decode()`) computes the **most-likely STATE SEQUENCE given the entire observation series**. It is a forward-backward dynamic-programming algorithm: the state assigned to bar t depends on observations BOTH before AND after t. This is appropriate for offline batch analysis (e.g., regime labelling of historical data) but is **non-causal** — it cannot be used for live trading or for any backtest that claims causality, because the state-label at bar t depends on future observations.

For causal use, implement the **forward (filter) algorithm**: compute `log P(state at t | observations through t)` and pick argmax over states. This uses only observations up to and including t — strictly causal.

**Why.** I1 audit 2026-05-16: built a per-asset HMM regime gate using `hmm.GaussianHMM.predict()` (Viterbi). The IS-frozen HMM training was correct (no parameter leakage), but the OOS state decoding via Viterbi pulled future observations back into past state assignments. The audit's causality smoke test (corrupt future closes; assert past returns unchanged) caught it immediately: corrupting closes at date T changed the gate at dates BEFORE T because the Viterbi back-pass propagated information from the corrupted suffix into the pre-T state path. 28 past returns differed across corrupted vs base runs — a clear look-ahead violation.

Fix: replaced `model.predict()` with a manual forward-algorithm implementation (`_causal_forward_states()` in `research/regime/hmm_gate.py`). The forward pass computes `log_alpha[t, j] = logsumexp(log_alpha[t-1] + log_transmat[:, j]) + log_emit[t, j]` then `argmax(log_alpha, axis=1)`. Strictly causal.

**How to apply.**

1. **Never use `hmmlearn.predict()` or `decode()` for live trading.** Both are Viterbi.
2. **For online/causal HMM inference, implement the forward filter manually.** Use the public `model.startprob_`, `model.transmat_`, and `model.means_/covars_` to compute log-likelihoods + the forward recursion in log-space (use `scipy.special.logsumexp`).
3. **Validate with the standard A10 causality smoke test.** Corrupt future closes from random date T; assert past returns are bit-exact unchanged. If the HMM-gated strategy fails this test, you're using Viterbi somewhere.
4. **This applies to ALL state-space models, not just HMMs.** Kalman smoothers (RTS pass) are non-causal; Kalman filters are causal. State-space backtests must use the FILTER, not the SMOOTHER.
5. **`scipy.special.logsumexp` is the right primitive.** Numerically-stable, vectorisable, single-line.

**Source.** I1 audit 2026-05-16. See `research/regime/hmm_gate.py::_causal_forward_states()` and the regression test `tests/test_hmm_gate.py::test_gate_is_is_frozen_oos_corruption_invariant`. The audit's causality smoke test caught this immediately on first run — the test design is what saved us.

### L51 — Per-asset HMM on raw daily returns degenerates to a no-op gate; richer features are required for meaningful regime detection

**Rule.** A 2-state Gaussian HMM fitted on raw 1-D daily returns of a single asset typically converges to a degenerate solution where one state covers 80%+ of bars and the other captures only brief vol spikes. Regardless of how the "trend-friendly" state is identified (autocorr / low_vol / high_mean), the resulting gate is ≈ 1 everywhere — i.e., it produces the SAME returns as the no-gate baseline. The "HMM regime filter" adds no information.

**Why.** I1 audit 2026-05-16: 13 pre-reg + sweep cells (varying training window, state count, state-id heuristic, seed, smoothing) all produced Sharpe within ±0.025 of the no-gate baseline (-0.2817). C1_canonical: -0.2816. C5_no_gate_baseline: -0.2817. Same value to 4 d.p. The HMM was numerically present but functionally absent. Three root causes:

1. **Single-state-dominant convergence.** With mostly-uptrending instruments (equity ETFs, gold), one state dominates and is always picked as "trend-friendly".
2. **Daily SNR is too low for state discrimination.** Signal ~0.04% mean vs noise ~1% stdev; the Gaussian emission model can't reliably separate bullish from bearish bars on a daily resolution.
3. **The regime structure lives in higher-dimensional features.** Realised vol, autocorrelation, drawdown depth, term-structure shape — none of these are visible to a raw-return HMM.

**How to apply.**

1. **Do not propose "HMM on raw daily returns" as a regime gate.** It will degenerate. Save the pre-reg overhead.
2. **If you want HMM-based regime detection, build richer per-asset features first.** Realised vol, lagged autocorrelation, multi-window momentum, drawdown depth — at least 3 features. This is a meaningfully different pre-reg from raw-return I1; cite it as I2 (or its own line).
3. **For univariate filters, prefer simpler primitives that don't claim to discover regimes.** Moving-average slope, vol percentile (B2d-style), drawdown threshold — these are direct, transparent, and don't degenerate silently.
4. **Sanity check ANY proposed regime filter against its own no-filter baseline as the FIRST cell of the audit.** If the filtered cell is within 1% of the baseline Sharpe across multiple parameter choices, the filter is a no-op — abort before running MC.

**Source.** I1 audit 2026-05-16 (`.tmp/reports/i1_ewmac_hmm_gate/result_log.md` §4.6). The full B2 → B2b → B2c → B2d → I1 escalation chain conclusively retired the universal-trend Carver EWMAC research line on cross-asset 21y data.

### L53 — Pass-1 gates Pass-2: skip MC when the headline Sharpe analytically cannot clear `CI_lo > 0`

**Rule.** Before launching the full Pass-2 MC + bootstrap pipeline (which is the dominant cost in any V3.6 audit), compute the analytic Lo (2002) standard error around the Pass-1 headline OOS Sharpe and check whether `headline_sharpe - z * SE_inflated > 0` is even plausible. If not (i.e., every cell's headline is so far below its own SE that no bootstrap could produce a positive CI lower bound), **skip Pass 2 and report RETIRED**. Pass-1 alone is sufficient evidence.

**Why.** I1 audit 2026-05-16: 13 cells produced Sharpe in [-0.31, -0.25] over ~1300 stitched-OOS bars. Pass 1 finished in minutes; Pass 2 then ran for ~50 minutes on C1's MC before being killed. No possible bootstrap could lift any of those cells to a positive `CI_lo`. The audit verdict was already determined by Pass 1 — Pass 2 was pure waste. The Lo SE formula `Var(SR_hat) = (1 + 0.5 * SR^2) / T`, inflated by `sqrt(block_size)` to account for block-bootstrap effective-sample-size reduction, is a cheap conservative gate.

**How to apply.**

1. **In every audit harness, after Pass 1 returns, call** `titan.research.framework.early_gate.pass1_can_clear_any_cell(cell_returns, periods_per_year=..., block_size=...)`. If `(any_can_clear, _)` is `False`, write the result log with `RETIRED` verdict and exit — do not launch `run_block_mc`.
2. **Log the per-cell SE + approx CI_lo via** `format_gate_report(per_cell)`. This goes in the result log §4.X so the skip decision is auditable.
3. **The gate is conservative** — it errs toward running Pass 2 when in doubt. A cell that marginally passes the gate may still fail Pass 2; that's correct behaviour (only obviously rejected strategies are skipped).
4. **Pre-screening DOES NOT replace Pass 2 for any cell that passes the gate** — the bootstrap CI is the real deployment gate; the analytic gate is only a "don't waste compute on obvious losers" filter.

**Source.** 2026-05-16 — built immediately after the I1 audit demonstrated the failure mode. See `titan/research/framework/early_gate.py` and `tests/test_early_gate.py`. The I1 reproduction test locks in that all 13 I1 cells (range [-0.31, -0.25]) would have been rejected analytically.

### L54 — Amortise IS-frozen model fits across MC paths: prefit ONCE, infer per path

**Rule.** When a strategy involves an expensive model fit (HMM, Kalman, regression, neural net) that V3.6's IS-frozen rule says should be fit on real IS data exactly once, the MC bootstrap must NOT re-invoke that fit on each synthetic path. Use the two-stage `prefit / infer` protocol: `prefit(real_is_df)` runs ONCE, `infer(synthetic_df, fitted_state)` runs per path.

**Why.** Naïve `strategy_fn(synthetic_df)` re-runs the entire pipeline (including fit) on every path. For I1: 31 instruments × 200 paths × 13 cells = 80,600 HMM fits ≈ 50 minutes serial. Amortised: 31 fits ONCE per cell, decode 6,200 times per cell. Decode is ~50x cheaper than fit, so a cell drops from ~4 minutes to under a minute. AND it's semantically MORE correct — re-fitting on synthetic IS implies the deployed strategy will re-train on each future bootstrap draw, which is not how production runs.

**How to apply.**

1. **For any strategy with a non-trivial model fit, define two callables:**

   ```python
   def prefit(is_df: pd.DataFrame) -> FittedState: ...
   def infer(synthetic_df: pd.DataFrame, state: FittedState) -> pd.Series: ...
   ```

   `infer` must be causal (L18 shift discipline, L50 forward-filter for state-space models).
2. **Call** `titan.research.framework.amortised_mc.run_block_mc_amortised(primary_close, cfg, prefit=..., infer=..., is_end_idx=..., periods_per_year=...)`. Returns the standard `McResult` — decision matrix consumes it identically to `run_block_mc`'s output.
3. **`is_end_idx` is the row count in `primary_close` that constitutes IS** (typically the end of the first WFO fold's IS or the sanctuary-aware IS boundary). `prefit` sees `primary_close.iloc[:is_end_idx]`.
4. **For stateless strategies (EWMAC, vol target, MR z-score) keep using `run_block_mc`** — there is no fit step to amortise; the standard path is optimal.
5. **For per-asset models (per-asset HMM, per-asset Kalman) `prefit` should return a dict keyed by asset.** `infer` looks up by asset name. Closure-captured state is shipped to workers via cloudpickle; if state is huge, run `n_workers=1`.

**Source.** 2026-05-16 — built alongside L53 after the I1 audit. See `titan/research/framework/amortised_mc.py` and `tests/test_amortised_mc.py`. The key invariant — `prefit` called EXACTLY ONCE — is locked in by `test_prefit_invoked_exactly_once`.

### L55 — Sanctuary Sharpe in regime-favourable windows is an upper bound, not a deployment claim

**Rule.** When the sanctuary holdout window happens to align with a regime that strongly favours the strategy's direction (e.g., long-gold during a gold rally, long-equity during a bull market), the sanctuary Sharpe can be 2-5x the stitched-OOS Sharpe. The `sanctuary_divergence_test()` flags this with `lucky_flag=True` when the sanctuary Sharpe sits in the top 5% of historical same-length rolling-window Sharpes. **When `lucky_flag=True`, the sanctuary Sharpe is the strategy's UPPER-BOUND performance under that regime, NOT a forward-deployment claim.** The deployment-relevant number is the stitched OOS Sharpe + bootstrap CI across the full WFO; the sanctuary number is informational about regime sensitivity, not about expected forward performance.

**Why.** bond_gold re-audit 2026-05-16: 24-month sanctuary (2024-04 → 2026-04) spanned the GLD rally from ~$60 to ~$280. Every cell with `threshold ≤ 0.50` had `sanctuary_percentile = 1.00` — i.e., historical 503-bar rolling windows in 2004-2024 NEVER produced a Sharpe as high as the sanctuary's. C1_canonical sanctuary Sharpe = +1.67 vs stitched OOS Sharpe = +0.66 (2.5x). C2_live_canonical sanctuary = +1.95 vs stitched OOS = +0.55 (3.5x). Citing the sanctuary number as "the strategy delivers Sharpe +1.7" would misrepresent the deployment expectation by ~3x.

This is the L17 pattern generalised: long-only equity-like exposure to a favourable regime inflates sanctuary far above the historical mean. The right interpretation: the strategy is real (CI_lo > 0 across rolling OOS + multiple favourable AND unfavourable historical regimes) but its forward expected Sharpe is approximately the stitched OOS Sharpe (~0.55-0.66 in this case), with upside in regime-favourable windows.

**How to apply.**

1. **In every result log §4 table, report BOTH the sanctuary Sharpe AND the sanctuary percentile** from `sanctuary_divergence_test()`. The percentile is the regime-favourability flag.
2. **In every promotion communication, cite the stitched OOS CI_lo as the deployment-relevant number** — NOT the sanctuary Sharpe. The sanctuary Sharpe is an upper-bound diagnostic.
3. **When `lucky_flag=True`, add an explicit caveat in the result log** that the sanctuary window is regime-favourable and the sanctuary Sharpe should not be cited as a deployment claim.
4. **For live deployment sizing, use the stitched OOS Sharpe in any leverage/vol-target calibration** — using the sanctuary Sharpe would over-size the position by a factor equal to the sanctuary-to-OOS ratio.
5. **Re-audit at a later date with the sanctuary window EXTENDED to include un-favourable regimes** to test whether the strategy survives outside the regime-favourable window. For bond_gold specifically, a 36mo or 48mo sanctuary that captures the 2022 Fed pivot (adverse for the IEF→GLD signal) would be more informative than the current 24mo.

**Source.** bond_gold V3.6 re-audit 2026-05-16. See `.tmp/reports/bond_gold_reaudit/result_log.md` §4.3 (sanctuary percentile column) and `.tmp/reports/bond_gold_reaudit/findings.md` "L17 regime-favourable sanctuary caveat" section. This generalises L17 (relative MC for long-only equity-class strategies) to the sanctuary axis.

### L56 — MA-crossover trend filters on long-only equity fail L17 relative MC; "drawdown protection" is regime-specific, not statistically robust

**Rule.** Long-only equity trend strategies that use moving-average crossovers (and similar threshold-based regime filters) as their drawdown-protection mechanism **systematically fail the L17 relative-MC test** — median drawdown reduction vs B&H equity collapses to ~1.0 under random-block bootstrap. The "trend filter protects against crashes" pitch is REGIME-SPECIFIC (works in actual historical paths where crashes have predictable autocorrelation structure) but NOT a statistically robust property under bootstrap. Combined with the inherent noise fragility of step-function MA signals (Varma noise axis = worst), these strategies cannot pass V3.6's 5-axis deployment matrix on equity indices.

**Why.** etf_trend_spy V3.6 re-audit 2026-05-16: 8-cell audit including the live `(slow_ma=150, exit_confirm_days=1)` AND the L52-recommended `(300, 5)`. All cells passed plateau (5% OOS spread), bootstrap CI (CI_lo +0.12 to +0.26), and Sharpe (+0.60 to +0.72 vs B&H +0.68) — but all failed L17 relative MC (median DD reduction 0.99-1.06 vs 0.80 gate; p_strategy_better 0.41-0.51 vs 0.50 gate) AND all failed Varma noise robustness (worst axis). Result: every cell received TIER_UNCONFIRMED → §3 selection rule yields RETIRED.

**Mechanism of failure:**

1. **Block bootstrap scatters crash bars** — 21-bar (1-month) blocks reshuffle. Real-world crashes have multi-month build-up patterns (volatility regime shifts, narrowing breadth) that the slow MA can detect with lag. Random-block bootstrap destroys those patterns; the MA can't preempt anything; strategy MaxDD ≈ B&H MaxDD.
2. **Step-function signals are noise-fragile** — when close ≈ sma, infinitesimal perturbations flip the regime. Varma's noise injection causes spurious entries/exits → cost drag + churn → Sharpe degrades sharply at the p5 noise level.
3. **The 5-axis matrix correctly identifies the trade-off** — bootstrap CI passes (best), DSR passes (best), sanctuary passes (best), but MC and Noise both fail (worst). 3 axes best → TIER_UNCONFIRMED, not DEPLOY.

**How to apply.**

1. **Do NOT promote any MA-crossover long-only equity trend strategy on the "drawdown protection" thesis under V3.6.** The L17 relative-MC gate will reject it.
2. **Two paths forward for the strategy class:**
   - **Direct B&H** — if the strategy's stitched-OOS Sharpe is within 10% of B&H equity Sharpe (typical for MA crossovers on SPY/QQQ/IWB), the simplest deployment is just buy-and-hold the underlying.
   - **Restructure the thesis** — re-frame the strategy as "Sharpe enhancement via vol-target" rather than "drawdown protection." Then evaluate against B&H-vol-targeted (not B&H raw), and demand a meaningful Sharpe gain (e.g., > 0.20 absolute). The L17 test isn't relevant for a pure Sharpe-enhancement thesis.
3. **For the 7-variant etf_trend class (SPY, QQQ, IWB, GLD, DBC, EFA, TQQQ),** the SPY audit's pattern is likely to generalise. Audit the highest-allocation variant next; if the pattern holds, RETIRE the entire class in bulk rather than running 6 more identical-outcome audits.
4. **Future trend strategies on equity indices** must propose an alternative mechanism for the drawdown-protection claim — e.g., per-bar volatility-state filter, cross-asset confirmation, options-based hedge overlay — that does NOT rely on a single MA crossover.

### L57 — Vol-target overlay max_leverage cap asymmetrically degrades Sharpe at high vol_target; hybrid sweep on (vol_target × estimator) reveals true optimum

**Rule.** When a vol-target overlay has a `max_leverage` cap (typical: 1.5x-2.0x), the cap binds asymmetrically as vol_target increases. At low realised-vol regimes, the strategy WANTS to scale UP beyond the cap → upside is truncated. At high realised-vol regimes, the strategy scales DOWN normally → downside is intact. The result: at high vol_target, the strategy delivers worse Sharpe than at low vol_target *because the cap binds in opposite directions on returns vs risk*. A canonical-selection sweep on the vol-estimator parameter alone (at fixed vol_target) will miss this — **the sweep MUST include vol_target as one axis**.

**Why.** GEM J5 hybrid re-audit 2026-05-16: J4 (2026-05-15) ran a 1D sweep on `vol_estimator_halflife` at fixed `ann_vol_target=0.10` and selected halflife=40 as the canonical (A1_ewma_hl40, promoted to LIVE). The J5 hybrid sweep extended to 2D `(halflife × vol_target)` and found:

- Sharpe declines MONOTONICALLY with vol_target: at vol_target=0.05, OOS Sharpe ≈ +1.00; at vol_target=0.10, ≈ +0.74 (-26%).
- The halflife axis is much flatter than vol_target — across halflife at fixed vol_target=0.05, spread is only 5%; across vol_target at fixed halflife=40, spread is 18%.
- L17 relative MC PASSES at vol_target=0.05 (38% DD reduction vs 60/40) but FAILS at vol_target=0.10 (12% reduction).
- 5-axis verdict: vol_target=0.05 cells earn DEPLOY; vol_target=0.10 cells (incl. J4 live) earn CONDITIONAL_WATCHPOINT at best.

The mechanism is the asymmetric cap: at vol_target=0.10 with max_leverage=2.0, the strategy frequently hits the cap during low-vol bull periods → upside truncated. At vol_target=0.05, the cap rarely binds → vol-targeting works as designed → Sharpe preserved.

**How to apply.**

1. **For any vol-targeted strategy with a leverage cap, the canonical-selection sweep MUST be 2D over (vol_target × estimator).** A 1D sweep on the estimator at a fixed allocator-mandated vol_target will miss the steeper vol_target sensitivity.
2. **Report both the unconstrained optimum AND the allocator-constrained optimum** in the sweep findings. The allocator's vol_target mandate is a SEPARATE constraint to be discussed AFTER the sweep + audit, not baked into the sweep design.
3. **When the unconstrained optimum lies at a materially lower vol_target than the allocator mandate,** the audit + findings should explicitly flag the trade-off: higher Sharpe + tighter CI_lo vs lower absolute capital deployment + freed-capital rotation question.
4. **For NEW strategies entering V3.6 audit**, this is now a checklist item: if the strategy includes a vol-target overlay with a leverage cap, ensure the pre-reg cells span at least 2 vol_target values.
5. **Reservation:** the same asymmetric-cap effect probably applies to OTHER constrained-position strategies (e.g., notional-cap fixed-size systems, drawdown-circuit-breaker strategies). Watch for similar patterns in future audits.

**Source.** GEM J5 hybrid re-audit 2026-05-16. See `.tmp/reports/sweep_gem_hybrid/findings.md` (sweep) + `.tmp/reports/gem_j5_reaudit/result_log.md` (audit) + `.tmp/reports/gem_j5_reaudit/findings.md` (migration memo). The J5 audit promoted `(halflife=60, vol_target=0.05)` to DEPLOY with OOS Sharpe +1.00 (vs J4 live +0.74) — a 34% improvement on the live strategy via L52 + L57 mechanisms.

### L58 — For strategies with complex filter machinery, run the signal-layer L52 sweep FIRST before auditing the full stack

**Rule.** When a strategy combines a base signal with multiple filter layers (regime filter, session filter, tier-grid sizing, hard-close timer, etc.), **the L52 hybrid sweep should be run on the SIGNAL LAYER (filters disabled) BEFORE auditing the full machinery.** If the signal-layer Sharpe is negative or zero with sample size adequate, the apparent edge from the full machinery is filter-dependent overfit-risk territory — a full V3.6 audit will either RETIRE the strategy or produce a Sharpe number that is filter-tuning-fragile.

**Why.** Wave A.3 mr_audjpy audit 2026-05-16: the live config `(vwap_anchor=24, entry_pct=0.95)` claims Sharpe +0.53 per the V1 corrected audit. A signal-layer L52 sweep (24 cells over `vwap_anchor × entry_pct` on 87k IS H1 bars, NO regime filter, NO session window, NO tier grid) found:

- The live config's signal-layer Sharpe is **-0.30** (CI excludes positive territory).
- The best individual cell `(vwap_anchor=36, entry_pct=0.98)` reached only +0.20.
- No plateau passes the L27 30% spread gate.
- The 0.83 Sharpe gap between the signal-layer audit (-0.30) and the V1 corrected audit (+0.53) is attributable to the live machinery: 4-tier grid, multi-scale regime filter (donchian H1/H4/D/W disagreement), session filter (07:00-12:00 UTC entries), 21:00 UTC hard close.

The risk: each filter is a tuning knob, and 4-5 knobs layered on a NEGATIVE base signal is the classic L43 + L46 overfit-accumulation pattern. The +0.53 in-sample number may not generalise.

**How to apply.**

1. **Before auditing any complex strategy under V3.6**, identify the base signal layer (the core entry/exit mechanic) and run the L52 sweep on that layer alone. Disable regime filters, session windows, tier grids, hard-close timers.
2. **If signal-layer Sharpe is ≤ 0 across the parameter plane** (no positive plateau), flag the strategy as "filter-dependent — likely overfit". Recommend de-allocation rather than running the full multi-dimensional audit.
3. **If signal-layer Sharpe is positive somewhere** but the live config is OFF the positive region, treat as a candidate for L52 hybrid re-audit (sweep → pre-reg → audit on full machinery). Pattern matches GEM J5 / bond_gold migration cases.
4. **If signal-layer Sharpe is positive AND live config is ON the positive region**, the live strategy's edge is at least partially in the signal layer (not purely in filters). Proceed with full-machinery audit normally.
5. **Sample-size guard.** This pattern is reliable only when the signal-layer sweep has adequate sample size. For mr_audjpy: 87k IS bars = 14 years of H1 = more than ample. For shorter histories (≤ 5y daily), the signal-layer sweep may itself be sample-limited and the "no edge" reading is less robust.

**Source.** Wave A.3 mr_audjpy signal-layer sweep 2026-05-16. See `.tmp/reports/sweep_mr_audjpy/findings.md`. The pattern generalises: for any strategy with > 3 filter knobs layered on a base mechanic, running the signal-layer sweep first is a cheap (~5 min) overfit-risk check that can preempt expensive multi-dimensional audits.

**Reinforcement from Wave A.5 (mtf) and Wave A.6 (mr_fx) 2026-05-16:**

### L60 — Cadence annualisation mismatch: `BARS_PER_YEAR[timeframe]` is asset-class-dependent

**Rule.** When computing Sharpe for an intraday timeframe (M5/M1/H1/H4), the `periods_per_year` argument MUST match the asset class's actual trading cadence, NOT a generic `BARS_PER_YEAR[timeframe]` lookup. The framework's `BARS_PER_YEAR["H1"] = 6048` assumes FX 24/7 trading. **For US equities RTH H1, the correct value is ~1764 (7 bars/day × 252 days)** — `sqrt(6048/1764) = 1.85x` Sharpe inflation if you use the wrong one.

**Why.** Wave B turtle audit 2026-05-16: the Wave B triage reported Sharpe +1.60 with CI_lo +0.35 on CAT H1 (live config). Investigation found triage used `BARS_PER_YEAR["H1"] = 6048` on US equity RTH data. Correct annualisation (1764) deflates the headline to +0.87 on the full series, +0.43 on WFO OOS. This is the same Sharpe magnitude as gold_macro, ic_equity_daily, and the etf_trend variants — not exceptional anymore.

**How to apply.**

1. **Never pass `BARS_PER_YEAR["H1"]` (or any FX-default constant) to `sharpe()` for US equity, US-listed ETF, or RTH-bound futures data.**
2. **Verify by counting bars in the parquet.** For each instrument, `actual_bars_per_year = len(df) / years_spanned`. Round and use that.
3. **Asset-class lookup table** (correct as of 2026-05-16):
   - FX (G10 majors, 24/7 except weekends): H1 ≈ 6048, M5 ≈ 72576.
   - US equity RTH (NYSE/NASDAQ, 9:30-16:00 ET): H1 ≈ 1764, M5 ≈ 21168.
   - US equity extended hours (4:00-20:00 ET): H1 ≈ 4032, M5 ≈ 48384.
   - CME equity index futures (almost-24h, with ~1h daily halt): H1 ≈ 5500, M5 ≈ 66000.
   - Daily ETFs (any timezone): D = 252.
4. **Pre-reg checklist for any new audit on intraday data**: state `periods_per_year` explicitly AND justify the choice with a 1-line note + actual-bars-per-year count.
5. **Retroactively check existing Wave B triages**: any H1/M5 strategy whose triage used the FX-default annualisation needs re-annualisation before the next audit phase.

**Source.** Wave B turtle full audit 2026-05-16. See `.tmp/reports/turtle_reaudit/findings.md`. The triage's +1.60 vs the audit's +0.43 OOS is a 3.7x apparent gap, of which ~1.85x is L60 (annualisation) and the remainder is the IS→OOS deflation plus selection bias.

### L62 — Sharpe-gap signature classification (V1 claim vs V3.6-correct reproduction)

**Rule.** When a V3.6 re-audit produces a Sharpe value materially below a V1 claim, the GAP magnitude is diagnostic of bug class:

| Gap (V1 − V3.6-correct) | Typical cause | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 0.5 − 1.5 | Methodology drift (cost model, annualisation, filter sequencing) | bond_gold canonical gap |
| 1.5 − 3.0 | Single look-ahead bug (e.g., same-bar shift, simple causality slip) | mtf gap 2.0 |
| 3.0 − 5.0 | Multiple compounding look-ahead bugs OR cherry-picked period | (none yet, theoretical) |
| 5.0 − 10.0 | Multi-TF composite-amplified L21 look-ahead | **ic_mtf gap 8-9 (avg)** |
| 10.0+ | Multi-TF + multi-signal stacking, OR fabrication / mechanism mismatch | (boundary case) |

**Why.** Same-bar look-ahead in a SINGLE signal typically inflates Sharpe by 1-3 units (the "mtf signature"). Multi-TF composites can compound look-ahead linearly with TF count × signal count, producing 8-15 unit inflation when 4 TFs × 2 signals × Spearman-sign-fit all leak together. This makes the GAP magnitude a useful prior on bug class: 2-unit gap = single causality bug, 8-unit gap = multi-TF composite amplification.

**How to apply.** When a V3.6 re-audit produces a large negative gap:

1. **First check (L63 protocol)**: run a V1-style baseline (full look-ahead permitted) on the same data. Does it reproduce the V1 claim?
2. **If YES** → bug is L21 (causality). Apply causality fixes (TF shift, IS-only fit, IS-only normalisation) and read off the "after" Sharpe — that is the strategy's TRUE economics. Verdict is RETIRE on the corrected number.
3. **If NO** → suspect bug in V1 mechanism, reproduction, or data. **Do NOT immediately retire** — verify reproduction first.

**Source.** Wave B ic_mtf 2026-05-16: 6 FX pairs, V1 +7-8 → V1-style +6.6 to +14.3 (recovers/exceeds V1) → causal alignment −1.0 to −0.2 (edge destroyed). 8-15 SR per-pair inflation from contemporaneous higher-TF alignment.

### L63 — Verify V1-style reproduction recovers V1 claim before concluding fabrication / mechanism mismatch

**Rule.** When a V3.6 re-audit fails to reproduce a V1 claim even at V1-style methodology (with all look-ahead freely permitted), **the FIRST priority is to verify the REPRODUCTION, not to conclude V1 is wrong**. Otherwise the framework risks false-negative retire verdicts.

**Why.** Wave B ic_mtf 2026-05-16: my first run produced negative Sharpe even at V1-style (Sharpe −0.34 to −2.61). I was within minutes of shipping a "fabrication / mechanism mismatch" verdict for V1's +7-8 Sharpe claim. **User challenge** ("check critically why catastrophic failure, are we doing something wrong") forced re-examination, which uncovered a data-loader bug in my pure-research module: FX H1 parquets store timestamp as a COLUMN (not the index — index is integer RangeIndex), and my loader was converting integers to nanoseconds-from-1970, scrambling multi-TF alignment so H1[i] was joined to H4[i] (different dates) instead of H1[date] being joined to H4[same date]. After fixing the loader, V1-style reproduction recovered the V1 claim (+6.6 to +14.3), confirming L21 as the actual bug class.

**How to apply.** When a V3.6 reproduction conflicts with V1, the priority order is:

1. **Run V1-style baseline** (full look-ahead permitted, no causality discipline). Does it reproduce V1's claim within ±20%?
2. **If NO**: suspect the reproduction. Check (in order):
   - Data loader integrity: index parsing, timezone handling, timestamp-column-vs-index conventions per asset class.
   - Multi-instrument / multi-TF alignment: are joins date-based or position-based?
   - Mechanism mismatch: re-read V1 strategy code line-by-line; the live strategy.py may differ from V1 audit script.
   - Period selection: V1 may have run on a specific date range; recompute on V1's window.
3. **If YES**: proceed with causality / methodology fixes to identify the V3.6-correct Sharpe.
4. **Only after exhausting (1)-(3)** should a "fabrication" or "mechanism mismatch" conclusion be drawn.

**Anti-pattern to avoid:** producing a strong-evidence "RETIRE — V1 audit untrustworthy" verdict without first proving the reproduction recovers V1 at V1's methodology. False-negative retires destroy legitimate strategy value AND erode trust in the framework's verdicts.

**Operational rule for solo workflow:** before publishing a "catastrophic gap > 5 SR units" retire verdict, write the gap-size and verdict in a separate sentence. If it would be surprising or controversial, write a single one-line sanity check (V1-style baseline) and run it before shipping. This is the L63 protocol.

**Source.** Wave B ic_mtf 2026-05-16. User feedback "check critically why catastrophic failure, are we doing something wrong" was the forcing function. Pattern locks in as discipline for all future V1-claim verifications.

### L64 — CONDITIONAL_WATCHPOINT path for borderline V1-era audits

**Rule.** When a V1-era V3.6 audit produces a strategy that:

- Passes L21 causality smoke (no look-ahead bug)
- Delivers positive OOS canonical Sharpe (≥ 0)
- Has costs that are not the binding constraint (gross − net ≤ 0.30)
- Has stable cell ranking (IS-best is approximately OOS-best)
- BUT fails the 5-axis matrix on CI_lo > 0 alone

the verdict should be **CONDITIONAL_WATCHPOINT (small-weight deploy, 6-12 month re-audit cycle)**, NOT RETIRE. The strict CI_lo > 0 gate at small fold counts is a confidence indicator, not a binary edge test, and is biased toward false-negatives at retail data scales.

**Why.** Bootstrap CI width scales as `1/√n_folds`. A genuine 0.5-Sharpe strategy on 4-5 expanding WFO folds will frequently show CI_lo around −0.3 simply from sampling noise — even when the underlying signal is real. Applied uniformly as a binary gate, this discipline produced 9 RETIRE verdicts out of 12 Wave A/B audits, with several borderline cases (gold_macro, turtle) showing positive OOS Sharpe across all cells. The cost of false-negative (retiring real edge that could have been deployed at small size) is non-trivial: it starves the portfolio of marginal-but-uncorrelated sleeves that aggregate to portfolio-level alpha.

**Industry reality check.** AQR / SocGen / Renaissance and similar managers deploy strategies at **Sharpe 0.5-1.2 with CI_lo near zero** and run hundreds of billions in AUM. Long-only momentum, value, quality factors live at Sharpe 0.3-0.7 in published research and remain profitable. Trend-following CTAs operate at Sharpe 0.4-0.8. A V3.6 framework that retires anything with CI_lo ≤ 0 on 4-5 WFO folds would reject most of academic asset pricing.

**How to apply.**

1. **After 5-axis matrix, classify each cell tier:**
   - **STRICT DEPLOY**: passes all 5 axes including CI_lo > 0 on stitched OOS.
   - **CONDITIONAL_WATCHPOINT**: passes L21 + cost-not-bottleneck + positive-OOS-Sharpe + cell-stability + (plateau OOS ≤ 80% OR IS plateau exists and was tightly clustered).
   - **RETIRE**: fails L21 OR negative-OOS-Sharpe OR cost-bottleneck OR no plateau-or-stability OR multi-instrument panel median < 0 AND no scope-lock acceptable.

2. **CONDITIONAL_WATCHPOINT deployment discipline:**
   - **Initial weight**: 25-50% of what STRICT DEPLOY for the same strategy would warrant.
   - **Re-audit cadence**: every 6-12 months on fresh sanctuary.
   - **Promotion**: if CI_lo crosses zero on re-audit → upgrade to STRICT DEPLOY.
   - **Demotion**: if new sanctuary Sharpe goes negative → downgrade to RETIRE.
   - **Live-vs-research parity check**: monthly; drift > 0.2 Sharpe → flag for early re-audit.
   - **Scope-lock**: if L61 multi-instrument panel showed CAT-specific edge, explicit single-instrument scope; do NOT extend to other instruments without per-instrument audit.

3. **Non-negotiable gates (NEVER relaxed):**
   - L21 causality smoke MUST pass.
   - Costs must not be the binding constraint (gross-net > 0.30 → RETIRE regardless).
   - V1-style baseline must NOT recover dramatically higher Sharpe than V3.6-correct (would indicate undetected L21 bug — L63 protocol).

**Application 2026-05-16 retroactive review:**

- **turtle**: RETIRED → CONDITIONAL_WATCHPOINT (CAT-scoped, C3_peak (entry=45, exit=20), 25-30% initial weight, 6-month re-audit). Justified by: L21 PASS, OOS Sharpe +0.69 (peak cell), stable cell ranking (IS-max == OOS-max), CI_lo -0.26 (tightest in cell grid), L61 scope-lock acceptable.
- **gold_macro**: RETIRE STANDS. Cell-ranking instability between IS and OOS (IS-best ≠ OOS-best, surface materially reshuffles) is a real fragility flag distinct from the CI gate. Not just CI noise; underlying signal structure isn't stable.
- **Other 7 Wave A/B retires**: RETIRE stands on strong evidence (L21 bugs proven in mtf/ic_mtf; L58 signal-layer negative on overwhelming samples in mr_fx/mr_audjpy; L56 rel-MC fail in 5 etf_trend variants).

**Anti-pattern to avoid.** Don't use L64 to rescue strategies that fail the non-negotiable gates. L64 is for strategies whose evidence is "marginal positive" — not "no evidence" or "ambiguous". The framework's job is still to prevent capital loss from broken strategies; L64 just acknowledges that "real positive but small" is a deployment-eligible state at small size, not a retire-trigger.

**Source.** User challenge 2026-05-16: "why are we retiring a lot of strategies while others claim they work and are profitable? are we missing or overlooking something critical?" Direct response to the asymmetric-cost-of-errors observation and the industry-deployment reality check. See `.tmp/reports/second_look_review/summary.md` for the gold_macro + turtle re-examination that produced this lesson.

### L65 — Risk-of-ruin assessment (formal P(ruin) before any CONDITIONAL or DEPLOY)

**Rule.** Before deploying any strategy at production size (STRICT DEPLOY or CONDITIONAL_WATCHPOINT), compute formal **P(ruin)** at the proposed deployment weight over a 12-24 month horizon. The MC MaxDD gate (per-strategy tail-risk proxy) is necessary but NOT sufficient — it doesn't translate to portfolio survival probability at deployed size.

**Why.** Wave A/B audits used `max_dd_threshold_pct=0.35, pass_prob=0.10` as the deployment risk gate — "probability of MaxDD > 35% must be < 10%". This is a per-strategy tail-risk indicator, not a portfolio-ruin gate. Two strategies that each pass this gate can simultaneously drawdown in a correlated regime, causing portfolio-level kill-switch trip with no per-strategy MaxDD breach. User challenge 2026-05-16: "are we assessing for risk of ruin?" — answer was NO, only proxies. L65 fills the gap.

The cost of false-negative on a ruin assessment is catastrophic (account zero or operator capitulation); the cost of false-positive is delayed deployment. Asymmetry favours adding the gate.

**Implementation.** `titan/research/framework/ruin.py` provides:

- `assess_strategy_ruin(returns, deployment_weight, portfolio_kill_threshold, horizon_bars, block_size, n_paths)` — single-strategy ruin probability at deployed weight.
- `assess_joint_ruin(strategy_returns_dict, deployment_weights_dict, ...)` — portfolio-level joint ruin across simultaneously-deployed strategies, preserves empirical cross-correlations via aligned-index block bootstrap.
- `RuinAssessment.passes()` — default gate:
  - `P(portfolio kill switch trips in horizon) < 1%`
  - `P(strategy DD > 50% at full size) < 5%`
  - `95th-percentile MaxDD at deployed weight better than -25%`

**Pre-deployment protocol (mandatory).**

1. **Run `assess_strategy_ruin`** at the proposed deployment weight on the OOS stitched return series. Must pass `.passes()` default gate.
2. **Run `assess_joint_ruin`** across all simultaneously-deployed strategies (existing LIVE + proposed new). Must pass at portfolio level.
3. **Report the assessment** in the §4 result log under a `§4.X RuinRisk` subsection.
4. **If gate fails**: either reduce deployment weight until it passes, OR delay deployment, OR identify a hedging strategy. Do NOT deploy at a weight that fails the ruin gate.

**Forward application 2026-05-16:**

- **turtle CAT C3_peak at 30% single-strategy**: assessment PASSES (P_kill=0.00%, P_dd_50%=0.00%, 95th-pct MaxDD = -7.98%, recovery ~287 H1 bars / 8 weeks). Sensitivity: gate fails at 50% weight (P_kill=2.8%); safe up to ~40% in isolation.
- **JOINT MC (GEM J5 70% + turtle CAT 30%)**: **FAILS** the joint gate (P_kill=1.10%, just over 1% threshold). The empirical daily-return correlation of +0.10 between strategies, combined with the higher-volatility 2018-2026 overlap window (COVID + 2022 + 2025), pushes the combined portfolio risk over.
- **REVISED DEPLOYMENT**: turtle CAT initial weight **20% (not 30%)** alongside GEM J5 @ 70%. P_kill = 0.30% (3.3x margin under gate), 95th-pct combined MaxDD = -9.74%. APPROVED.
- **Single-strategy L65 audit of GEM J5 alone on 2018-2026 window**: P_kill=2.00% — FAILS. The recent-regime period is more turbulent than the full 2003-2026 history (P_kill=0.55% PASS). **Open question**: is current LIVE GEM J5 deployment L65-compliant on the regime we're deploying INTO? Action item separate from turtle decision.

**Critical lesson from joint MC**: single-strategy L65 is necessary but NOT sufficient. Joint L65 across LIVE + proposed strategies is the binding gate for new deployment. Correlation in the 0.05-0.15 range can push combined risk over the threshold even when each strategy passes alone. Always run joint MC before deploying new strategies alongside existing LIVE ones.

**Anti-patterns.**

- "MC MaxDD gate passed, so we're safe." Wrong frame; that gate is per-strategy tail risk, not portfolio survival.
- "We have a portfolio kill switch, so ruin is bounded." Kill switch protects against catastrophic continuation, but a -15% portfolio loss IS the ruin condition for many operators (capitulation point, redemption requests, operational viability).
- "The strategy backtest looks fine at full size." Backtest at full Kelly is irrelevant; production is typically deployed at fractional Kelly (10-30%) for survivability. Verify at the production weight.

### L66 — Baseline selection must match strategy thesis (V3.7 framework evolution)

**Rule.** Different strategies need different baselines. The V3.6 L17 test (strategy vs same-instrument buy-and-hold) was applied universally — it is the CORRECT baseline only for "risk-reducing long-only equity strategy". For other strategy theses, L17's B&H baseline is a category mismatch that systematically favors B&H over any active strategy.

| Strategy thesis | Correct baseline | Pass criterion |
|---|---|---|
| Risk reduction (trend filter, MA crossover, defensive gate) | Vol-matched B&H of underlying | Strategy MaxDD < B&H at equal vol; Calmar > B&H; Sortino > B&H |
| Alpha generation (cross-sectional momentum, factor) | CAPM/FF residual regression | t-stat(alpha) > 2.0 after DSR deflation; IR > 0.5 |
| Pure return enhancement (turtle, breakout) | Cash + PSR(0) > 0.95 | DSR > 0.5; bootstrap CI_lo > 0 |
| Diversifier (trend overlay, VRP, regime gate) | Portfolio with-and-without the candidate | Δ Sharpe of joint portfolio > 0.10; Δ MaxDD > −5pp |
| Cross-asset rotation (GEM, dual momentum) | 60/40 SPY/IEF (or All Weather) | 7+/10 on the portfolio decision matrix (L67) |

**Why.** L17 systematically favors B&H for long-only equity strategies because:
1. **Equity Risk Premium leakage**: in-market X% of time → capture X% of ERP. Strategy must add > (1-X)% × ERP just to match B&H CAGR.
2. **Missed-recovery asymmetry**: trend rules flat at the bottom miss the sharpest recovery bars.
3. **Transaction costs compound**: strategy pays cost-per-trade × trades; B&H pays once.
4. **Selection bias** (DSR adjusts but doesn't eliminate): strategy was chosen from many candidates; B&H wasn't.

For long-only equity strategies on equity instruments, this 1-2 SR-unit headwind is real and structural. The L17 verdict against such strategies is mechanically correct but doesn't answer the relevant question (does the strategy ADD VALUE to a portfolio?).

**How to apply.**

1. **Pre-reg template addition**: every new pre-reg directive declares `baseline_class` from the table above. Default for cross-asset momentum: `cross_asset_rotation`. Default for single-instrument trend: `risk_reduction`.
2. **L17 dispatch**: implementation of `run_relative_block_mc` selects benchmark function based on baseline_class:
   - `risk_reduction` → vol-matched B&H of underlying (lever B&H down to strategy vol)
   - `alpha_generation` → CAPM/FF residual
   - `pure_return` → cash (zero return)
   - `diversifier` → portfolio-without-candidate
   - `cross_asset_rotation` → 60/40 SPY/IEF
3. **Audit findings template**: §4 result log must report which baseline was used and the rationale.

**Forward application:**
- GEM J5 audit was correctly using 60/40 baseline (per L57 note). Existing audit verdict stands.
- turtle CAT audit used CAT B&H (L17 default) — this was a CATEGORY MISMATCH. Re-evaluated at portfolio level (L67), turtle ADDS value to GEM+turtle vs 60/40 portfolio test.
- gold_macro and 5 etf_trend variants RETIRED on L17 vs own-asset B&H — re-evaluation under L66 unlikely to change the verdict (they failed plateau/CI gates beyond just L17), but the principle is: L17 vs own-asset B&H alone is not deployment-blocking.

**Source.** User challenge 2026-05-16: "why is buy-and-hold less risk of ruin than any of the strategies we are developing? The main aim is to minimise risk while keeping profitable compared to buy-and-hold." Research synthesis revealed the framework was running the wrong test. See `.tmp/reports/joint_evaluation/framework_evolution_v37.md`.

### L67 — Portfolio-vs-benchmark 10-metric matrix is the deployment gate (not individual-strategy L17)

**Rule.** Individual-strategy L17 (vs same-instrument B&H) is a USEFUL DIAGNOSTIC for standalone strategy quality. **It is NOT the deployment gate.** The deployment gate is the portfolio-level 10-metric matrix vs a diversified benchmark (60/40 SPY/IEF default). A new strategy candidate is DEPLOY-eligible iff adding it to the existing portfolio improves the 10-metric verdict.

**The 10-metric portfolio decision matrix** (implemented in `research/portfolio/joint_evaluation.py`):

| # | Metric | Formula | Gate |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Sharpe (annualised, cash-relative) | (μ − r_f) / σ × √252 | P > B by ≥ 0.10 |
| 2 | M² vol-matched excess (Modigliani-Modigliani) | SR × σ_B | M²_P − M²_B ≥ 0.015 |
| 3 | Sortino | (μ − r_f) / downside_dev | P > B |
| 4 | Calmar | CAGR / |MaxDD| | P − B ≥ 0.30 |
| 5 | MaxDD reduction | 1 − MaxDD_P / MaxDD_B | reduction ≥ 0.30 |
| 6 | CVaR-95 reduction (Rockafellar-Uryasev) | 1 − CVaR_P / CVaR_B | reduction ≥ 0.25 |
| 7 | CDaR-95 reduction (Chekhlov-Uryasev) | 1 − CDaR_P / CDaR_B | reduction ≥ 0.30 |
| 8 | Risk-of-ruin (joint, 1y, 15% threshold) | P(NAV DD > 15% in 1y) | P < B |
| 9 | Information Ratio (Grinold-Kahn) | excess.mean() / excess.std() × √252 | IR ≥ 0.30 |
| 10 | Excess > 0 stability across MC samples | % MC samples where excess > 0 | pct ≥ 0.60 |

**Verdict logic:**
- 8+/10 PASS → **PORTFOLIO_DEPLOY** (real upgrade vs benchmark).
- 5-7/10 PASS → **PORTFOLIO_CONDITIONAL** (marginal; watch tail metrics).
- < 5/10 PASS → **PORTFOLIO_REJECT** (prefer benchmark; consider portable-alpha overlay design instead).

**Why.** The user's challenge 2026-05-16 revealed that V3.6 was running the wrong test. Empirical demonstration:

- Individual strategy on own-asset B&H: most strategies fail (the L17 mechanic explained in L66).
- Portfolio (GEM 70% + turtle 20%) vs 60/40 SPY/IEF over 7.9 years: **7/10 PASS** including 64× lower P(NAV DD > 15%), Sharpe +0.95 vs +0.74, MaxDD −13.7% vs −22.3%.
- Same portfolio test on GEM J5 alone (23-year history): 6/10 PASS — confirms GEM J5 LIVE is a real upgrade vs 60/40.

The portfolio-level test answers the relevant question. Individual-strategy L17 was a category mismatch.

**How to apply.**

1. **Every new strategy candidate** goes through TWO gates:
   - **Strategy quality gate** (V3.6, modified per L66): WFO + sanctuary + MC + DSR + class-appropriate baseline.
   - **Portfolio inclusion gate** (V3.7, NEW): does adding this strategy improve the 10-metric portfolio matrix vs 60/40?

2. **A strategy can pass quality gate but fail portfolio gate** if it's too correlated with existing strategies (no diversification benefit) or too vol-thin (no risk-budget value). RETIRE such strategies.

3. **A strategy can fail quality gate but pass portfolio gate** if its uncorrelated diversification value exceeds its standalone weakness. PROMOTE such strategies to CONDITIONAL_WATCHPOINT at small weight.

4. **Re-run portfolio matrix on EVERY** LIVE allocator change. If existing portfolio falls below 5/10 → flag for review.

5. **Quarterly portfolio-vs-benchmark report**: even with no allocator change, recompute the matrix every quarter using the most recent 1-year window. Detect drift.

**Forward application:**

- Current portfolio (GEM 70% + turtle 20%): **PORTFOLIO_CONDITIONAL (7/10)**. APPROVED. Maintains current deployment.
- Future candidates (fx_carry, ic_equity_daily, orb, pairs): test individually under L66-appropriate baseline, THEN test portfolio addition under L67.
- If the joint portfolio drops below 5/10 PASS on quarterly re-run → urgent review.

**Anti-pattern to avoid.** Don't use individual-strategy L17 as a hard retire criterion. L17 is a quality diagnostic; L67 is the deployment decision. A strategy that fails L17 vs own-asset B&H may still be portfolio-additive.

**Implementation reference.**
- `research/portfolio/joint_evaluation.py` — the 10-metric harness.
- `titan/research/metrics.py` — added `cvar()` + `cdar()`.
- `titan/research/framework/ruin.py` — existing L65 joint MC.

I still do not understand why buy and hold is less risk of ruin than any of the strategies we are developing." Research synthesis + portfolio-level evaluator implementation revealed the framework's structural gap. The portfolio-level test answers the question definitively. ### L68 — Always pass `--env-file .env.docker` for compose operations that recreate or start containers

**Rule.** Every `docker compose up`, `docker compose restart`, or `docker compose run` command that touches a service in the Titan stack MUST include `--env-file .env.docker`. The compose stack relies on environment variables (`TWS_USERID`, `TWS_PASSWORD`, `IBKR_ACCOUNT_ID`, `UNIVERSE`, `IBKR_CLIENT_ID_GEM`, etc.) that are sourced from `.env.docker`, not from process env. Omitting the flag causes the IBKR Gateway container to start with blank credentials and hang indefinitely at "Setting password".

**Why.** V3.7 Phase 2 cutover (2026-05-17 23:43 UTC): my first `docker compose up -d --force-recreate titan-portfolio` was missing `--env-file .env.docker`. This caused `--force-recreate` to cascade to ib-gateway (via `depends_on`), which restarted without TWS credentials. Gateway hung at "Setting password" for 5+ minutes. Healthcheck failed 8 consecutive times → marked unhealthy → titan-portfolio dependency failed → didn't start. Recovery required re-running with the proper flag.

The compose CLI emits `level=warning msg="The 'TWS_USERID' variable is not set. Defaulting to a blank string."` but this is easily missed in the cascade of "Container ... Recreate / Started" output during a normal operation.

The docker-compose.yml file header already documents this convention (`Bring up: docker compose --env-file .env.docker up -d --build`), but the convention is human-discipline-only, not enforced.

**How to apply.**

1. **Every interactive compose command in operator's terminal or runbook**:
   ```bash
   docker compose --env-file .env.docker up -d --build
   docker compose --env-file .env.docker restart titan-portfolio
   docker compose --env-file .env.docker up -d --force-recreate titan-portfolio
   ```
2. **Wrapper script** (recommended): create `scripts/dc.sh` that prepends `--env-file .env.docker` automatically:
   ```bash
   #!/bin/bash
   exec docker compose --env-file .env.docker "$@"
   ```
   Then `./scripts/dc.sh up -d --force-recreate titan-portfolio` is foolproof.
3. **Pre-flight check before compose ops**: `grep -c '^TWS_USERID' .env.docker` should return 1. If 0, abort — credentials missing.
4. **Watchdog auto-restart** uses the `restart: unless-stopped` Docker policy which inherits the container's existing env (set at create time). The flag matters at CREATE / RECREATE time, not at restart time of an already-configured container.

**Detection at runtime.**

- Gateway hung at "Setting password" for > 2 min = almost certainly missing env vars
- Compose output line `level=warning msg="The 'TWS_USERID' variable is not set"` = definitive evidence
- `docker exec titan-ib-gateway env | grep TWS_USERID` returns empty = no credentials

**Recovery** (if hung):
```bash
docker compose --env-file .env.docker up -d --force-recreate ib-gateway titan-portfolio
```
This re-creates both containers with the correct env. Takes ~30 seconds for healthy login.

**Source.** V3.7 Phase 2 cutover 2026-05-17. See `directives/V3.7 Phase 2 Cutover Log 2026-05-17.md`. Pattern locks in as a non-negotiable operational convention.

### L69 — Noise fragility is a distinct closure mechanism from regime artifact (CI_lo) or sample-size; cleaner data can falsify the wrong diagnosis

**Rule.** When a multi-variant audit produces clean positive Sharpe on every cell AND positive sanctuary AND positive bootstrap CI_lo on the best-noise-mid cells AND a DEPLOY verdict only on the gross baseline, the closure mechanism is **worst-case noise robustness** — not regime artifact, not sample-size, not signal failure. Per the V3.7 5-axis decision matrix, `CONDITIONAL_WATCHPOINT` promotion requires `noise_axis == "best"` (i.e., the worst-case 50% noise injection still passes the 30% degradation gate). A signal that is positive in expectation but fragile to feature noise (instrument idiosyncrasy + costs) sits in a separate diagnostic class.

**Why this distinction matters.** A "RETIRE" verdict on noise fragility opens different revival paths than a "RETIRE" on regime artifact or CI_lo:

- **CI_lo bottleneck (L46)** → wait for more data; the underlying edge is statistically inconclusive.
- **Regime artifact (L48)** → strategy worked on one regime/universe and not another; do not deploy without a regime-detection layer.
- **Noise fragility (L69)** → signal is real but mean-driven, not robust-driven; rescue paths are (a) per-asset regime gating to selectively trade only the high-conviction subspace (motivates I1 HMM), (b) lower-cost execution to widen the gross-vs-net gap, (c) different aggregation (e.g., weight cells by realised noise robustness within the audit).

**Worked example.** B2 EWMAC family closures (2026-05-15 → 2026-05-17):

| Audit | Universe | Bars | Folds | Failure mode | Closure mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| B2 | IBKR commodity 24sym 3y | 760 | 2 | CI_lo barely negative (-0.044) | **L46 sample-size** |
| B2b | yfinance broad 31sym 21y | 5574 | 60 | C1 Sharpe -0.28, plateau 37% | **L48 regime artifact (possibly L40-aggravated)** |
| B2e | IBKR x-asset 11sym 9y | 2789 | 30 | All cells positive, CI_lo>0 on 2 cells, noise=mid failing worst-case | **L69 noise fragility (this lesson)** |

B2e independently falsifies B2b's "universal EWMAC decline" reading: on clean Databento futures (no L40 contamination) across 4 asset classes, the signal is positive on every cell with strongly positive sanctuary. So **L48 should NOT be read as "EWMAC universally retired"** — it should be read as "yfinance commodity-ETF contamination + universe heterogeneity produced an artefact, but cleaner cross-asset data still doesn't clear the noise gate."

**How to apply.**

1. **Always run noise robustness** as the 5th axis. A strategy that clears CI_lo, DSR, MC, and sanctuary but flunks worst-case noise is in this class — it's NOT a simple RETIRE.
2. **Report the closure mechanism in the result log**, not just the verdict string. The Retirement Registry entry must distinguish L46 / L48 / L69 so future revival has the right anchor.
3. **L69 motivates regime gating.** When you see L69, the natural next step is per-asset regime detection that filters EWMAC down to the windows where worst-case noise robustness holds. This is the I1 HMM hypothesis specifically.
4. **Cleaner data can resolve diagnosis ambiguity.** If a "regime artifact" suspicion is based on data of known quality issues (yfinance + L40 + survivorship), re-running on cleaner data (Databento + roll-stitched) and getting a DIFFERENT failure mode (L69) means the original L48 closure was at least partially mis-diagnosed.

**Source.** B2e IBKR cross-asset 11-symbol 9y audit (2026-05-17). See `directives/B2e Sweep Report 2026-05-17.md` and `research/ewmac/run_b2e_audit.py`.

### L70 — Multi-feature regime gate is the rescue path for L69 noise fragility (B2 family partially revived)

**Rule.** When an EWMAC-class strategy fails the V3.7 worst-case noise gate (mean_pass=True, worst_pass=False; noise_axis = "mid") but otherwise passes CI_lo / DSR / MC / sanctuary axes, a multi-feature regime gate trained on a CROSS-ASSET MACRO FEATURE PANEL (vol z-score, term spread, credit spread, dollar, drawdown velocity, regime indicator) with PER-ASSET trend-friendly state mapping can flip the noise axis to "best" without degrading sanctuary Sharpe. **This is the operational counter to L69.**

**Why this works (mechanism).** Worst-case noise injection corrupts the un-gated forecast uniformly across bars. The regime gate masks the bars where the asset is NOT in its trend-friendly state — and those are typically the bars where the un-gated forecast has the lowest signal-to-noise ratio (regimes the asset doesn't trend in). By screening these bars out, the realised return distribution becomes less sensitive to noise injection on the un-gated forecast, because the un-gated forecast in screened-out bars is not used. The gate doesn't add information; it screens out low-signal bars.

**Pre-requisites for the gate to work (i.e., to escape L51 simultaneously):**

1. **Feature panel must be multi-feature.** Per-asset HMM on 1-D daily returns degenerates (L51). The regime panel used in I1v2 has 7 macro features (`data/i1_regime_panel.parquet`).
2. **Features must be causally constructed** (rolling z-scores using only past data, no centred windows).
3. **State path must be CAUSAL forward-filtered**, not Viterbi (L50).
4. **Per-asset trend-friendly mapping is IS-frozen**: each asset's set of trend-friendly states is determined IS-only, then applied unchanged to OOS.
5. **Pre-pre-reg diagnostic gate:** before running the audit, fit the HMM on IS-only and verify state occupation max ≤ 65% (or skewed but interpretable e.g. bull state 84%), seed-stability ≥ 0.80, and mean run-lengths ≥ 5 days. If degenerate, the panel itself is insufficient.

**Worked example.** I1 v2 audit (2026-05-17) on B2e's 11-symbol IBKR cross-asset universe:

| Metric | B2e C5 (no gate) | I1v2 C6 (gated) |
|---|---:|---:|
| OOS Sharpe | +0.4863 | +0.5215 |
| CI95 lo | -0.008 | **+0.049** |
| Sanctuary Sharpe | +1.0934 | +1.0964 |
| Noise axis | mid (worst_pass=False) | **best (worst_pass=True)** |
| Verdict | TIER_UNCONFIRMED | **DEPLOY** |

The gate (2-state HMM + per-asset mean-return state ID + 5-day median smoothing) flipped the noise axis without touching sanctuary Sharpe. Gate fractions per asset varied from 0.0 (6J, never gated on — no global state has positive yen IS mean return) to 1.0 (SI/GC gold-class, both states trend-friendly), with Treasuries (ZN/ZB) gated-on ONLY in the crisis state (21%) — economically interpretable flight-to-quality behaviour.

**How to apply.**

1. Whenever a strategy lands at noise_axis = mid (passing mean but failing worst-case noise), do NOT retire immediately. Test a multi-feature regime gate.
2. Pre-pre-reg diagnostic first: confirm the regime panel admits non-degenerate states (escape L51 by construction).
3. Pre-reg the gate cells before running the audit. Standard 5-axis matrix applies unchanged.
4. The gate is COMPLEMENTARY to the un-gated strategy — combine into a single live strategy, not separate ones (the gate adds no compute cost at runtime; it's a single global state at each bar).
5. **L48 / L69 are not universal retirements** — the EWMAC signal is real on cross-asset data when properly regime-gated. Future "EWMAC family RETIRE" verdicts should explicitly check L70 first.

**Source.** I1 v2 audit (2026-05-17). See `directives/Pre-Reg I1v2 Multi-Feature HMM Regime Gate 2026-05-17.md` §4, `research/regime/hmm_gate_v2.py`, `research/ewmac/run_i1v2_audit.py`. Pre-pre-reg diagnostic in `research/exploration/diagnose_i1_v2_regime_hmm.py`.

### L71 -- A frozen ML artefact is a build artefact of its feature pipeline; treat it like a compiled binary, not immutable data

**Rule.** Whenever a strategy's signal layer is a frozen joblib/pickle model loaded at runtime, its companion feature module is part of the model's effective ABI. If the feature module changes (columns added, removed, renamed, or semantics changed) after the model was trained, the model is **silently invalidated** -- the runtime crash is the only warning, and that warning only fires when the strategy is added to a live container.

**Required guards.**

1. **Embed feature names in the artefact.** Both at training time and at on_start. Then assert `set(model.feature_names) <= set(build_features_output.columns)` and crash with a clear "feature drift detected" error rather than letting model.predict() raise a less informative shape mismatch.
2. **CI integration test.** `tests/test_<strategy>_model_feature_compat.py` -- loads each model file in `models/`, runs a 1-row prediction with current `build_features()`, asserts no exception. Drift caught at PR time, not at deployment.
3. **Treat the model file as a build artefact, not immutable data.** When the feature pipeline evolves, the model must be regenerated. The CI test in (2) enforces this by failing on drift.
4. **Document the model's training context in its artefact.** `trained_at` timestamp, `feature_names` list, and ideally a content-hash of the feature module's source at training time. This makes "is the live code compatible with this model?" a 5-second check rather than a multi-hour debug.

**Worked example.** ml Wave C audit (2026-05-17):

| | |
|---|---|
| Model file | `models/meta_model_EURUSD_H1_20260403_104654.joblib` |
| Model trained | 2026-04-03 (avg_auc 0.69, threshold 0.6, 20 features) |
| Strategy file | `titan/strategies/ml/strategy.py` (loads model at on_start) |
| Feature module | `titan/strategies/ml/features.py` |
| Drift status | **12 of 20 features the model expects are absent** from current `build_features()` (`primary_signal`, `confluence_score/abs`, `adx_proxy`, `rsi_14/21`, `rsi_overextended`, `hour_utc`, `is_london/new_york/overlap`, `day_of_week`) |
| Live impact | Not yet -- ml is not in `v37_live`, so the crash has not happened in production. But if ml were added to a STRATEGY_SET tomorrow, the container would crash on first model.predict() after warmup. |
| Verdict | RETIRE_BLOCKED until either (a) retrain on current features, or (b) restore the older features.py revision that matches |

**How to apply.** Future joblib-based strategies must:

- Store `feature_names` + `trained_at` in the artefact (existing convention).
- Implement on_start feature-compat check (raise + log on mismatch).
- Add a CI test in `tests/` that loads each `models/*.joblib` and runs a 1-row prediction. Failing CI when an unrelated feature edit breaks a model is the entire point.

### L72 -- Classification AUC > 0.55 is NOT a deployment signal; always close the loop with strategy-return 5-axis matrix

**Rule.** A binary meta-classifier with sanctuary AUC modestly above 0.50 (e.g., 0.55-0.60) is INSUFFICIENT evidence to deploy. The classification-layer signal must be converted into a (-1, 0, +1) trading signal, applied with V3.6 causal `.shift(1)` discipline, and the resulting strategy returns must clear the full 5-axis matrix (CI_lo > 0, DSR survives N-trial deflation, MC drawdown gate, sanctuary divergence test, noise robustness=best). AUC and Sharpe are NOT interchangeable -- many AUC-positive strategies fail Sharpe-positive because (a) the strategy trades constantly (high transaction-cost drag), (b) the prediction confidence is too weak to size positions efficiently, or (c) the wins-vs-losses payoff asymmetry is unfavourable even with > 0.50 hit-rate.

**Worked example.** ml top-6 5-axis audit (2026-05-17). The L61 multi-asset grid identified 6 assets with sanctuary AUC > 0.55 at 6h XGBoost (best mean 0.542). A naive read would say "MARGINAL deployment candidate." The 5-axis matrix on the per-asset strategy returns showed:

| Asset | Sanc AUC | Sharpe | CI95 lo | Verdict |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| IEF | NaN | -1.15 | -2.12 | RETIRE |
| EUR_GBP | 0.588 | +0.03 | -0.45 | RETIRE |
| EUR_USD | 0.561 | +0.09 | -0.34 | RETIRE |
| GLD | 0.562 | -0.76 | -1.36 | RETIRE |
| USD_CHF | 0.562 | +0.14 | -0.51 | SUSPECT |
| EUR_CHF | 0.563 | +0.28 | -0.26 | SUSPECT |

**0/6 deployment-eligible.** Every CI_lo is negative. The strategy is "active" on 96-99% of bars (degenerate constant-trading at the symmetric threshold), so transaction costs and noise dominate the marginal AUC edge. The V3.6 5-axis framework correctly filtered out what AUC alone would have approved.

**Why this matters.** Without L72, the L61 grid's "MARGINAL_BY_ASSET" verdict would have been an attractive intermediate state that could justify a "let's shadow-deploy and see" decision. The 5-axis matrix is what makes that decision honest. AUC and Sharpe are different metrics targeting different questions:

- AUC asks: "given two random bars, can the model order them correctly by future class?"
- Sharpe asks: "across all bars, does the strategy's realised PnL distribution have favourable risk-adjusted mean?"

A model can be modestly good at the first question while still being a terrible trader because: cost drag, position sizing inefficiency, asymmetric payoff structure, or simply that the AUC edge is too small to overcome transaction friction.

**How to apply.**

1. Whenever a classification audit produces an AUC-positive result, ALWAYS convert to strategy returns and run the V3.6 5-axis before any deployment recommendation. "AUC > 0.55, candidate for deployment" is an INCOMPLETE statement.
2. **L72 + L71 together** are the ML deployment gates: artefact-vs-feature-pipeline compatibility (L71) AND strategy-return 5-axis matrix (L72). Either failing kills the deployment claim.
3. For meta-classifiers in particular, also report the **fraction_active** stat. If > 90% of bars are signal-active, the strategy is approximately a constant-direction bet, not a selective trade, and costs will likely dominate.

**Source.** ml top-6 per-asset 5-axis audit (2026-05-17). See `.tmp/reports/ml_top6_5axis/result_log.md` + `research/ml/run_ml_top6_5axis.py`. Builds on L61 expansion grid (commit 2033042) which discovered the AUC signal, and L13/L14 disciplines that pre-committed the model.

### L73 -- Strategy-registry cutovers must diff against EVERY per-strategy registry, not just the top-level list

**Rule.** When the live-strategy registry changes (e.g. cutover from `champion_portfolio` to `v37_live`), perform a diff against every parallel registry that tracks per-strategy state. These cross-module operational registries don't trip the test suite because they're not behavioural; they're operational invariants that go silent or noisy on mismatch. The diff isn't optional -- it's the only mechanism that catches the regression before the next operator notices a missing alert or a false-positive page.

**Concrete registries to diff on every cutover** (Titan-IBKR as of 2026-05-19):

1. `scripts/run_portfolio.py::STRATEGY_REGISTRY` (per-strategy class + config_kwargs)
2. `scripts/run_portfolio.py::PORTFOLIOS` (the cutover target itself, e.g. `v37_live`)
3. `titan/utils/bond_gold_decisions.py::LIVE_CONFIGS` (D5 shadow-decision check tracks these)
4. Any passive ops strategy that reads its own per-strategy registry (e.g. reconciliation, daily_summary)
5. `config/risk.toml::[allocation]` weights (allocator config must match the live set)
6. `.env.docker` env vars referencing strategy names

**Why this isn't covered by existing tests.** A new test could check "every strategy in `v37_live` has a class in `STRATEGY_REGISTRY`" -- but the regressions we hit aren't about missing keys; they're about **extra keys** in adjacent registries that reference strategies no longer running. `LIVE_CONFIGS` listing `bond_equity_ihyu_cspx` is fine syntactically; it's just operationally stale. The test surface for "stale auxiliary registry" requires comparing the live set to every per-strategy registry on every cutover, which is what a manual diff is for.

**Worked example.** V3.7 multi-strategy cutover, 2026-05-17. The aggregate `v37_live` list was correctly built (`gem_j5_canonical`, `turtle_cat_c3peak`, `ewmac_regime_i1v2_c6`). Three adjacent registries were not updated in lockstep:

1. `daily_summary` not added to `v37_live` -> **Slack morning/night summaries went silent from 2026-05-14 onwards.** State file proof: `{"morning": "2026-05-14", "evening": "2026-05-13"}`. Fix: PR #5.
2. `reconciliation` not added to `v37_live` -> **position-drift watchdog silent.** Fix: PR #5.
3. `LIVE_CONFIGS` not pruned of `bond_equity_*` -> **D5 watchdog fired false-positive Slack alerts** within seconds of `reconciliation` restoration in PR #5. D5 matches positions by symbol not strategy_id, so GEM's 16 CSPX was misattributed to the non-running `bond_equity_ihyu_cspx`. Fix: PR #6 (default `shadow_check_enabled=False` for `v37_live`).

The `LIVE_CONFIGS` docstring already said in the source *"Update in lockstep with run_portfolio.py STRATEGY_REGISTRY when the live config changes"* -- the lockstep failed because the cutover lacked a checklist.

**How to apply.**

1. Add a "cutover diff" section to every multi-strategy cutover directive (e.g. `directives/V3.7 Phase 2 Cutover Log 2026-05-17.md`). The section enumerates every per-strategy registry above and confirms each is in sync with the target.
2. When pruning a strategy from the live set, also prune (or explicitly mark inactive) every adjacent registry that references it. When adding, same in reverse.
3. After the next force-recreate, watch the first 5 minutes of container logs for:
   - Missing `<X>Strategy: RUNNING` for any passive ops strategy you expected.
   - Spurious `detected drift` / shadow-decision warnings citing strategies that aren't in the registry.
4. A useful CI-time check: pytest that walks `titan/strategies/*` and `titan/utils/*_configs.py`-style modules, collects all strategy names referenced, and warns when a name appears in an auxiliary registry but is not in any `PORTFOLIOS` value. Soft-fail (warning) -- the noise is non-trivial for strategies retired vs paused.

**Why it's a class of bug, not a one-off.** The same root cause produced all three regressions in this session and was the same one that's caused similar bugs across V3.x cutovers historically (mr_audjpy retired but referenced; samir_stack paper validation set). It's a systemic property of cross-module registries in a system that grows by adding strategies and shrinks by retiring them: addition is loud (you write the code and merge a PR); retirement is silent (you delete one line and the auxiliary references don't fail to compile). Cutovers are the moments where the difference shows up at runtime.

**Source.** PR #5 + PR #6 + this incident response (2026-05-19). See commits `19b9de2` (passive ops restore), `8a4d4a1` (D5 default off), and prior `18edd54` (bar.close_time_as_datetime hotfix from same V3.7 cutover class of regression).

### L74 -- Carry-premium-to-Sharpe is `yield * time_in_market / vol`, NOT `yield / vol`; a trend-filtered carry strategy is only on ~50% of the time

**Rule.** When estimating how much a constant carry / yield component contributes to a trend-filtered strategy's Sharpe, the correct expression is `annual_yield * time_in_market / strategy_vol`, where `time_in_market` is the fraction of bars the position is on (not a constant 1.0). A trend-filtered carry strategy with an SMA filter is typically in market 40-60% of the time, which halves the premium-to-Sharpe boost relative to a naive `yield / vol` calculation. Failing to account for this leads to overstated economic-Sharpe estimates and miscalibrated decisions about whether to deploy.

**Why it matters.** The Wave B 2026-05-16 audit of fx_carry left an explicit caveat: *"swap premium (~+3-4% AUD-JPY rate diff) adds ~+0.6 SR if priced."* This estimate used the naive `yield / vol` math (3% / 8% vol target ≈ 0.375, rounded up). The audit-time pure-price Sharpe was +0.29 with CI_lo -0.23, failing the L66 baseline gate. Operators correctly held the deployment pending closure.

**Worked example.** fx_carry L65+L67 closure on 2026-05-19. Re-running with explicit per-bar swap accrual (held-fraction-scaled, not constant-scaled):

| Annual swap yield | Live Sharpe | CI_lo | Premium boost |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 0.0% (pure-price) | +0.2888 | -0.227 | (baseline) |
| 2.0% | +0.4458 | -0.068 | +0.157 |
| 3.0% | +0.5243 | +0.012 | +0.236 |
| 4.0% | +0.6027 | +0.093 | +0.314 |

The actual premium-to-Sharpe slope is +0.078 per 1% yield, not the +0.125 (`1/8% vol`) the naive math predicts. At the central historical assumption (3% AUD-JPY differential), the boost is +0.24, not the +0.6 the Wave B caveat claimed. The strategy still clears the L66 gate at 3% (CI_lo > 0), but with much smaller economic margin than the caveat implied.

**Diagnosis: the AUD/JPY position is on roughly half the time** (SMA-50 trend filter keeps it out of half the sample). So per-bar swap accrual is roughly `yield/252 * held_fraction ≈ yield/252 * 0.5`, halving the Sharpe contribution.

**How to apply.**

1. Whenever a carry-class strategy's economic Sharpe is being estimated and the strategy has a trend or volatility filter that gates entry, compute the per-bar swap accrual explicitly: `swap_accrual = held_position * (annual_yield / bars_per_year)`. The held series is whatever the strategy carries in `held = position.shift(1).fillna(0)` -- the same series the pure-price log return is multiplied by.
2. Report the carry premium as a sensitivity sweep, not a single point estimate. The realised AUD-JPY policy differential ranged from ~1% (2014-2016 BoJ-Fed coordinated easing) to ~4-5% (2024-2025 BoJ-divergence regime) over the 2010-2025 audit window. A single "3%" point estimate would be a fabrication; sweep [0%, 2%, 3%, 4%] and let the L65/L67 verdicts emerge.
3. Caveat estimates in pre-reg directives must use the correct formula. The Wave B caveat (~+0.6 SR boost) was a factor-of-2 overstatement. Future carry audits must use `yield * time_in_market / vol`.

**Reverse implication.** Carry strategies WITHOUT a trend filter (always in market) DO get the full `yield / vol` boost. A carry strategy that uses a market-regime gate to stay out 50% of the time is paying for its trend protection with a halved economic edge.

**Source.** fx_carry AUD/JPY L65+L67 closure (2026-05-19). See `.tmp/reports/fx_carry_l65_l67/result_log.md` + `research/portfolio/run_fx_carry_l65_l67.py`. Builds on Wave B 2026-05-16 audit and corrects its caveat.
